## 1. Misiunea — unde a ajuns

**Evoluție completă:** De la template/ritual de bifat → filtru → scrisoare către sine → punct de întoarcere.

**Versiunea finală** (salvată în `To be posted/2026-08-03`):

> Most founders don't write their mission.
> They carry it. In instinct — the deal that felt wrong, the client they turned down without being able to explain why, the direction they refused when everyone else said yes.
> It works. Until the pressure starts. Until someone makes you an offer that pays well but feels wrong. Until the team pulls in three directions and you can't remember which one you chose.
> Nobody writes their mission for the business.
> They write it for themselves. For the moment they're sitting across from the tax office. For the person in the room with a different agenda. For the first time someone makes a compromise sound reasonable.
> Not on the wall in the hallway. On the desk. In front of them.
> The point they return to when everything else is pulling them somewhere else.
> Write it down now. Before someone else decides what your flame is worth.

**Insight cheie:** Misiunea nu e scrisă pentru afacere — e pentru versiunea ta de dinainte de compromis. E punctul tău de întoarcere.

## 2. Mordor și Valorile

**Concept:** Nimeni nu vrea să meargă mai adânc pentru că abstractul simte ca Mordor — fără fund, fără lumină, fără garanția că te întorci cu ceva util.

**Răsturnarea:** **În Mordor sunt valorile.** Exact ce caută toată lumea, dar acolo e ultimul loc unde se uită.

> Nobody wants to go deeper.
> The founder doesn't. The designer doesn't. One step back and it feels like Mordor — no floor, no light, no way to know if you'll come back with anything useful.
> So they start where it feels solid. The logo. The colors. The tone of voice. Things you can see, approve, and ship.
> But everything is built on sand.
> What's in Mordor? The only thing that makes any of it real.

## 3. Personalitatea VINE din Valori (Nu se alege)

> Most brands don't have a personality problem.
> They have a values problem they're trying to solve with tone of voice.
> The designer does exactly what they're supposed to do — they work with what they're given. If what they're given is vague, they fill the gap with what they know: form, tone, aesthetic choices that feel right.
> The result looks like a brand. It's scenery.
> Personality isn't chosen. It's derived — from values that are real, specific, and not interchangeable with any other company in the market.

## 4. Omul din Mordor — Jumătatea de Pagină

**Povestea reală:** Un tip de ~150kg, cu păr slinos, autist, fumător, care dispărea zile întregi și venea cu jumătate de pagină care putea opri orice proiect.

**Ce era el:** Strategul adevărat. Nu cel care face deck-uri — cel care gândește. Toată lumea îl evita, patronul îl ținea ca talisman.

**Sfârșitul:** Fie pleca din demnitate, fie era ținut la munte de patron. Când rolul lui a fost dizolvat, toată lumea livra mai repede — dar nimic nu mai însemna nimic.

> Every agency had one.
> The person who disappeared for days and came back with half a page.
> No visuals. No deck. No progress to show.
> Just half a page that could stop an entire project in its tracks.
> The room called it obstruction. Called him difficult. Some called it charlatanism...
> When the role dissolved, the designers designed faster, the copywriters wrote more, everything got delivered on time. It just stopped meaning anything.

**Tu la 23 de ani:** Vroiai să fii **el**, nu **ca el**. Greutatea pe care o avea jumătatea lui de pagină — autoritatea fără spectacol. Acum ai ajuns acolo pe propriul tău drum, cu propria ta jumătate de pagină.

## 5. Toți se Declară Strategi (Dar Nu Au Fost în Mordor)

> Everyone knows they need a strategist now.
> So they become one.
> A course. A framework. A few books on brand positioning. Some templates downloaded from someone who downloaded them from someone else.
> Then they open a deck and call it strategy.
> What they don't know is that strategy isn't a skill you learn. It's a pattern you recognize — after enough projects that went wrong for reasons nobody could explain...

## 7. Planul de Serii (Purpose, Values, Mission)

**Notă:** Nu e "mesaj, misiune, valori" — ci **Purpose, Values, Mission** — în abstract, nu execuție. Fără mesaj, voce, personalitate. Acelea sunt consecințe, nu fundație.

**Stadiu:** Schemă, neîncepută. Se construiește când vii cu un gând.
