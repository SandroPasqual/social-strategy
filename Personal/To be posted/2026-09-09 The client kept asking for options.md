The client kept asking for options. More options. As if the answer was hidden in the volume.\n\nOptions are a safety mechanism for indecision. The more there are, the harder it is to commit to any of them. The client isn't looking for the right answer — they're looking to spread the risk of choosing wrong.\n\nA single strong recommendation carries more weight than a dozen safe alternatives. It says you know what you're doing. Options say you're not sure.\n\nThe best clients don't ask for options. They ask for your judgment.
