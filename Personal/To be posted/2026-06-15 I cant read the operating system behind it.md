I can't read the operating system behind it.

I sometimes try to reconstruct what I was actually doing as a kid. Not to remember fondly — to understand. Like studying field notes from a species I can no longer observe directly.

The external facts are still there. A city full of unguarded construction sites. Communist-era materials everywhere — scaffolding, sand, half-poured concrete, wire. Technically public, which meant belonging to no one, which meant belonging to us entirely. We built things, broke things, climbed things that had no business being climbed.

But the internal architecture of those days — I can't access it. What were the thoughts? How did decisions form? What filled the silence between events?

I know the movement. I can't read the operating system behind it.
