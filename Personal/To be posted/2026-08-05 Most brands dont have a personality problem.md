Most brands don't have a personality problem.
They have a values problem they're trying to solve with tone of voice.
The designer does exactly what they're supposed to do — they work with what they're given. If what they're given is vague, they fill the gap with what they know: form, tone, aesthetic choices that feel right.
The result looks like a brand. It's scenery.
Personality isn't chosen. It's derived — from values that are real, specific, and not interchangeable with any other company in the market.
When values are clear, personality follows without a decision. When they're not, you're decorating an absence.
