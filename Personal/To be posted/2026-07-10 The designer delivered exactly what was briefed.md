The designer delivered exactly what was briefed.

The brief was a paragraph. A reference image. A feeling.

The designer worked with what they had. Produced something competent. The client approved. Nobody said anything.

Months later, the brand still doesn't hold. Not because the work was bad. Because the work was answering a question nobody had properly asked.

We've been on both sides of that exchange. Giving a brief that's really a wish. Receiving one and filling the gaps with instinct.

The work usually looks right. It rarely lands right.
