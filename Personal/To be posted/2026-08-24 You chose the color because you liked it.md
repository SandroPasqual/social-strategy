You chose the color because you liked it. The market responded differently.\n\nColor is not preference. It's a signal that carries context you can't control. What looks confident to you reads aggressive to your buyer. What feels safe reads forgettable.\n\nThe difference between personal taste and strategic choice is whether you're willing to separate what you like from what the market needs to see. Most founders never make that distinction.\n\nThe palette that works is rarely the one you would have chosen for yourself.
