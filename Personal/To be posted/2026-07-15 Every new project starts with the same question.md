Every new project starts with the same question: where do we begin?

Not because people lack talent. Because the starting point is rarely clear.

A designer reinvents the foundations. A copywriter writes without knowing who the client actually is. The client reviews without knowing what to approve. Good people with no shared entry point.

The ones who pause before starting — who ask what the center is before anyone moves — those are the ones whose work holds. Not because they're slower. Because they know that direction is more important than speed.

And direction can't be borrowed. It has to be established.
