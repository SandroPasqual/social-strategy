Everyone knows they need a strategist now.
So they become one.
A course. A framework. A few books on brand positioning. Some templates downloaded from someone who downloaded them from someone else.
Then they open a deck and call it strategy.
What they don't know is that strategy isn't a skill you learn. It's a pattern you recognize — after enough projects that went wrong for reasons nobody could explain, after enough briefs that looked complete and produced nothing, after enough beautiful executions that solved the wrong problem.
You can't shortcut that. You can only pretend you have.
