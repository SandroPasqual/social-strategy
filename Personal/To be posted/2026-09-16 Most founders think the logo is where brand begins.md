Most founders think the logo is where brand begins.

So they hire a designer. References are requested. Pinterest is consulted. Something gets built. A PDF arrives.

It looks like a brand. It might even look good.

That night, something feels off. Not wrong enough to say anything. Just — not quite right. Not quite theirs.

What nobody told them is that the designer can only build from what they were given. And what they were given was a folder of references and a deadline.

The two steps below the logo were never taken.
