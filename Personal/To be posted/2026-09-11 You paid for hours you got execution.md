You paid for hours. You got execution. The thinking was free. It showed.\n\nHourly billing sells time. But time isn't the product — judgment is. When the price is tied to hours, the incentive is to fill them with activity, not thought. The thinking gets compressed into whatever fits before the clock starts running.\n\nThe result is work that's correct but thin. It answers the brief without advancing the position.\n\nYou got what you paid for. The question is what you were actually buying.
