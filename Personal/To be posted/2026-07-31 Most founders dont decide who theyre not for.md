Most founders don't decide who they're not for.
They find out after.
After the call that felt off. After the project that drained everything. After the client who paid but cost more than the invoice.
In the early days it didn't matter — you needed the work. The problem is the business grew around those clients. The pricing, the processes, the communication — all calibrated to people you wouldn't choose again.
And then growth feels dangerous. Because scaling means more of what's already exhausting you.
You already know who you're not for. You've met them.
