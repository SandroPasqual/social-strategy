You briefed a designer. You gave them a reference. You're hoping they read your mind.\n\nThey won't. They'll deliver what the reference shows, not what you meant. The reference is a crutch — it replaces the harder work of saying what the problem actually is.\n\nA good designer doesn't need a reference. They need a problem that's been articulated well enough that the solution becomes inevitable.\n\nIf you're giving references, you're not briefing. You're hoping.
