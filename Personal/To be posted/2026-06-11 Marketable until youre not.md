Marketable. Until you're not.

I noticed something I wasn't prepared for. The way we remember people — it's always the last version. The tired one. The one diminished by time or illness or distance. The young person, the sharp one, the one full of questions and bad decisions and energy — that one is gone. Even the people who knew them then can no longer fully access it.

Memory degrades. The early versions don't survive.

So I started writing. Not for publication. Not for approval. For the people who will know me only in the last chapter — so they have something from the middle of the book. So they know how I thought, what bothered me, what I found funny, what I couldn't let go of.

Not a legacy. Just evidence that I was here, like this, when I was.
