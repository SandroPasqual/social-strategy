Every agency had one.
The person who disappeared for days and came back with half a page.
No visuals. No deck. No progress to show.
Just half a page that could stop an entire project in its tracks.
The room called it obstruction. They called him difficult. Some called it charlatanism — what exactly had he been doing in there?
What he'd been doing was the only work that made everything else make sense.
When he left — or when the role was quietly dissolved because clients couldn't see the value — the designers designed faster, the copywriters wrote more, and everything got delivered on time.
It just stopped meaning anything.
