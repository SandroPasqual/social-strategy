'What style do you want?' You've asked this a hundred times. You've never gotten a useful answer.\n\nBecause it's the wrong question. The client doesn't know what style they want — they know what feeling they're trying to create. Style is a proxy for something deeper. Asking for it directly skips the only part that matters.\n\nThe question isn't what it should look like. The question is what it needs to do.\n\nMost briefs fail at the first question.
