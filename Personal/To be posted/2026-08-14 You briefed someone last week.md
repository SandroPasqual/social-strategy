You briefed someone last week. They nodded. You're not sure they understood. Neither are they.\n\nYou assumed the nod meant alignment. It meant they didn't want to look slow. Now the work is coming back wrong — not because they're incompetent, but because the gap between what you said and what they heard was never closed.\n\nThe difference between a briefing and a conversation is whether you're willing to hear that you weren't clear. Most people aren't.\n\nThey nod because nodding is easier than saying I don't get it yet.
