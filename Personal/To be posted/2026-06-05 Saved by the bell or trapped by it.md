Saved by the bell. Or trapped by it.

When the phone buzzes mid-conversation, something happens that we don't like to name honestly.

We call it multitasking. We call it staying responsive. We call it a lot of things that make it sound like a skill.

But watch someone do it. The eyes shift. The attention splits. The person in front of you becomes a placeholder while the notification gets its moment. The conversation doesn't pause — it degrades.

We've designed a world where the person in the room competes with the person who isn't. And the person who isn't always wins — because they bring the promise of something new, while the person in front of you carries the weight of the already known.

The phone buzzes. We answer it. We don't say goodbye to the conversation. We just let it die without a ceremony.
