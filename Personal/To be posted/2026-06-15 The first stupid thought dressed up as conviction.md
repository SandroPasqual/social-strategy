The first stupid thought, dressed up as conviction.

Something has happened to our ability to digest complexity. Not suddenly — slowly, then all at once.

After an event, we used to sit with it. Turn it over. Ask what actually happened, what it meant, what we might do differently. That processing took time and silence and a willingness to be wrong.

The phone offered something simpler: one vector. One feed, one angle, one tone. No need to correlate a thousand signals. The vector decides. You follow.

And once you've followed long enough, the vector becomes instinct. You stop questioning it because it feels like you. You call it intuition. Experience. Faith. The first thing you felt.

The first thing you felt is now the last word.

We don't go back to check. We don't revise. We defend the original read until we run out of breath — holding the line on something we formed in three seconds before we knew anything.

The first stupid thought, dressed up as sacred.
