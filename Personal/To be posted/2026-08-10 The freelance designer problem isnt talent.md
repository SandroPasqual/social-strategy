When you're the strategist, the account manager, the bookkeeper, and the executor — you can't think at the level the work requires. The friction doesn't just waste time. It compresses the quality of judgment.
AI is removing most of that friction. The administrative burden that used to require a full agency apparatus can now be handled by systems. That changes the math on what a small partnership needs to function at a high level.
The structure of the future isn't a solo operator with better tools. It's a small group of people with complementary judgment — strategist, designer, copywriter — sharing infrastructure, dividing the work that requires human presence, and letting AI handle everything else.
Pentagram worked because partners shared the cost of infrastructure. That model is now accessible to three people instead of twenty.
The question isn't whether to build this. It's who belongs in the room.
