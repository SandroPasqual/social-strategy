The brand didn't run out of options. It ran out of courage.

Using leather because it's the best material is a position. Using it because your entire identity is built on it — and changing would mean admitting the foundation has shifted — is something else.

You're not being sold a product. You're being sold the brand's reluctance to evolve.

The tell is always the same: when the thing they're protecting is no longer the quality, but the story they've told about the quality.

That's not heritage. That's the old substance running out.
