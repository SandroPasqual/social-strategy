You've worked with creatives who need total freedom. They're expensive. They're also guessing.\n\nTotal freedom isn't a creative advantage — it's the absence of a brief dressed up as vision. The best work comes from constraint, not from a blank page. Anyone who demands total freedom either doesn't know what they're solving or doesn't want to be held to it.\n\nThe most expensive creatives aren't the ones who charge more. They're the ones who make you pay twice — once for the work, once for the revision cycle that brings it back to where it should have started.
