What happens in that room stays in that room.

Not because there's nothing to show. Because the work requires access to what isn't working — the pitch that keeps falling flat, the gap between what was built and how it reads. That is vulnerable information.

Some of the best work I've seen never appears in a portfolio. Some of the strongest transformations happen in conversations that were never recorded. The trust required to be honest about what isn't working is rare — and it's earned, not requested.

When you find someone who doesn't turn your uncertainty into their content, you hold onto them.

Not because they can't show what they know. Because they understand that some rooms don't have windows.
