Your website doesn't need a dark mode. It needs a personality.

Most brands don't use mixed themes.
They use random contrast:
Dark hero → "We're edgy!"
Light text → "We're readable!"
Then back to grey soup → "We ran out of ideas."

But real mixed themes aren't about "best of both worlds."
They're about drama with purpose.

✅ Dark isn't "cool." It's a spotlight.
Use it to make one thing impossible to ignore.

✅ Light isn't "safe." It's a relief.
Use it so people can breathe after the show.

✅ Switching isn't decoration. It's rhythm.
Like a song that drops into silence — then explodes.

And if your "impactful hero section" looks the same as every other fintech site?
You're not bold. You're on brand autopilot.

So — what's one site that uses light and dark not for style… but to make you feel something?
