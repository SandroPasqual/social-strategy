Your brand color isn't about what you like. It's about what works.

You don't pick it because it's "your favorite."
You pick it because it pops on a phone screen,
survives a bad printer,
and doesn't turn into mud on a dark background.

Here's how to stop guessing:

- Test it on black. Test it on white.
If it disappears in either place, it's not ready.

- Forget "pretty." Aim for "noticeable."
The best colors aren't the boldest — they're the ones you can't miss.

- Match it to your brand's personality — not your taste.
Want to feel trustworthy? Blue's not magic — consistency is.
Want to feel bold? Red won't save a weak message.

Check it in the wild.
On a business card.
On a phone screen in sunlight.
On a PowerPoint.
If it still holds up — you're close.

So — what's one brand that just nails their color — not because it's fancy, but because it never fails?
