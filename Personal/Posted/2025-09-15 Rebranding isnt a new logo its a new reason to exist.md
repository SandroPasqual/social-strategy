Rebranding isn't a new logo. It's a new reason to exist.

Most companies get it wrong. They change the colors, fonts, visuals — and call it a day.

But the real problem?
Still untouched.

Customers don't care about your fresh design if the story behind it is broken.
You can't polish a lie.

So before you hire a designer, ask:

✅ Fix clarity: Why do you exist? For whom?
If you can't answer in one sentence, you're not ready.

✅ Fix alignment: Does your team live the brand — or just sell it?
Culture eats branding for breakfast.

✅ Fix direction: Are you building momentum — or just repainting the walls?
Motion isn't progress.

If you're only changing how you look…
it's decoration.

If you're changing how you connect…
it's rebranding.

So — what's the worst "rebrand" you've seen?
The one that changed the look… but left the soul broken.
