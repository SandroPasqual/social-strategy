I look at a logo in a utilitarian way.

Before anything else, I picture it on a business card, a poster, a website — working seamlessly across every touchpoint. Because a great logo isn't just beautiful; it's functional.

At its core, it needs to be impactful, easy to digest, and above all, memorable.

And here's the thing: color doesn't matter at this stage — even though that's my expertise. A strong logo must work in black. It must work reversed. It must hold its power without gradients, effects, or context.

What really matters? The FORM. The mark itself.

It has to be scalable, recognizable, and timeless. Because recognition isn't created by color — it's built by shape, by silhouette, by simplicity.

A logo isn't decoration.
It's identity.

And identity starts with structure.

What logos do you think nail this balance of simplicity and strength?

But here's what no one asks:
Does your logo actually say who you are?
Not just what you do — but the effort behind it.
The late nights. The stubborn belief.
The values you refuse to compromise.

It tells your story — quietly, clearly, without shouting.
