Adobe has built an empire on subscriptions, updates, and, frankly, a lot of user frustration. Most of their software comes with a hefty price tag, locking out many creators from tools they need.

And then there's Fresco — a genuinely excellent drawing and painting app, available for FREE. It's almost shocking.

Is it generosity? A nod to artists who have long been at the mercy of Adobe's ecosystem? Or is it just competition — Procreate, Krita, Clip Studio forcing their hand?

Whatever the reason, it's a rare instance where a major software company gives without immediately taking. For artists, that counts.

I like to think it's the former. Deep down, I suspect it's the latter. Either way, we get to draw, paint, and create — without paying upfront.
