Who is the Brand Book actually for?
(Spoiler: It's not for the graphic designer).

If your Brand Book is sitting in a folder, looking pretty, you're doing it wrong.
It's not a portfolio piece. It's a strategic weapon.
But a weapon is useless if the right people don't know how to wield it.

So, who is it for and what should they DO with it?

1. Leadership: It's your compass. Use it to make decisions — big, strategic ones — that are actually aligned with your brand's core. Stop guessing. Start leading with purpose.

2. Marketing Teams: It's your rulebook. Use it to brief agencies, guide copy, and launch campaigns that sound like they come from one company, not ten different departments.

3. New Hires: It's your culture manual. Use it to onboard people into why we do what we do, not just how. Turn employees into authentic brand ambassadors.

In short: it's for anyone who makes a decision that touches the customer. It's the single source of truth that stops the internal chaos.

So, I'm curious: who has access to your Brand Book, and how do you make sure it's actually used?

If you're not sure — let's talk.
