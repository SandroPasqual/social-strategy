A year ago, AI would tell you the complementary color of red is green.

It wasn't a mistake. It was a matter of context.

For centuries, artists learned that green is the complement of red. That model is based on pigments, on the practice of mixing colors on a palette. It works perfectly for painting.

But for the screen you're reading this on, it's a lie. In the digital world, the true complement of red is cyan. That's the science of light, not pigment.

A year later, AI has learned the distinction. It has evolved.

But what about us? Do we distinguish between a truth for an artist's palette and a truth for a screen? Or do we keep relying on outdated models?

When was the last time you made a decision based on context, not just old knowledge?
