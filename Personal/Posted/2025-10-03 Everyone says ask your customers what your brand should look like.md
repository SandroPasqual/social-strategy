Everyone says: "Ask your customers. They'll tell you what your brand should look like."

Here's the trap:
Customers don't give you clarity. They give you contradictions.
One says "make it bold."
Another says "keep it soft."
Someone loves the blue. Someone hates it.

Sincerely — has anyone ever run a survey on colors or fonts and ended up with a consistent identity?
What you get is chaos.

Customer voices matter — but as raw material.
It's the designer's job to filter, to find the shared emotional core, and to turn contradictions into a system.

That's the difference between a mood board built on opinions and a brand identity built on coherence.
