When brand colors clash with interface colors

Have you ever seen a green button in an app and assumed everything was "good"… only to realize it was just the brand logo color?

This is exactly what happens when brand colors overlap with semantic colors — the ones that send clear messages in your interface: error, warning, success, info.

🔹 Semantic colors aren't chosen for aesthetics — they're functional. Red = danger, yellow = warning, green = success, blue = info.
🔹 Brand colors are chosen for emotion and visual identity.

When a brand color is too close to a semantic color, confusion happens:

🔹 A brand green might look like "success" even when it's not.
🔹 A brand red might read as "error" even if nothing is wrong.

Elegant solution:

Shift the entire semantic color set slightly (just a few degrees on the color wheel) so the relationships between semantic colors stay intact, but they no longer clash with the brand palette.

The result:

✅ The brand stays recognizable
✅ The interface remains clear and intuitive
✅ Users don't get "false signals" just because of color
