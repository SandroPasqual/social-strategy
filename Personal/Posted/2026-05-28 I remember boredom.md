I remember boredom.
Not the restless kind that demands to be fixed. The kind where you'd sit by the window for an hour, watching for the bus, and that was enough. You were just there, with your thoughts, and your thoughts were company.

Boredom back then was the gap where everything else grew. Music found you there. Drawing happened there. Ideas that needed silence to form — they lived in that gap.

I still know what to do with my own thoughts. That part didn't leave.

But the window is gone. The bus wait is gone. The moment I feel the edge of that silence now, the phone appears. Not because I decided. Because it's faster than deciding.

An addict doesn't choose the dose. They just reach.
