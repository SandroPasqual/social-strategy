Stop breaking your idea just to look "lean."

"Test your idea. Validate your idea. Tweak it. Break it."
Sounds cool on a slide deck.
In reality? It kills more startups than it saves.

Here's why:

Testing too early = asking the wrong people.
Validating with random feedback = designing by committee.
Tinkering forever = never launching.
Breaking your own idea = losing the courage to believe in it.

Execution beats validation theater.
Clarity beats endless feedback loops.

And no, a Facebook poll won't tell you if your logo, product, or brand works.

If you're serious, stop treating your idea like a fragile prototype that needs constant approval.

Build it. Shape it. Own it.
Then let the market decide.
