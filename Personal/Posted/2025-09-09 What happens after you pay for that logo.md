What happens after you pay for that logo? (Spoiler: you're only halfway there.)

Most founders celebrate when the files arrive.
Vector. Check.
Invoice paid. Check.
"Now we're branded."

❌ No.

You've just been handed a flag — but you haven't built the country it represents.

A logo isn't a brand.
It's a symbol without a system.
And symbols die fast without consistency.

Here's what actually matters after delivery:

✅ Build the rules, not just the mark
One page. Two. Doesn't matter.
Define spacing, size limits, icon variations.
No more "I resized it in PowerPoint."

✅ Test it where it lives
Favicon? Business card? Dark mode?
If it fails at 16px, it's not ready.
Perfection means nothing if it's not functional.

✅ Own it legally — now
Start the trademark.
Your symbol is only yours if the law says so.

✅ Plan for evolution, not revolution
Will this logo still work in 5 years?
Can it adapt — or will you rebrand every 3 years like it's fashion?

A vector file isn't the finish line.
It's the starting gun.

So — when your logo was delivered… what was the very next thing you actually did?
