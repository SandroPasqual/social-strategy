Your brand color isn't about emotion. It's about control.

You didn't pick blue because of "trust."
You picked it because your competitor did — and you didn't want to stand out.

You didn't choose green because of "growth."
You chose it because it looked good on a moodboard next to a forest photo.

And that "primary color test on your phone screen"?
Nice ritual.
But if your brand vanishes in dark mode or clashes with system alerts,
it's not a strategy.
It's decoration with good intentions.

A real brand color doesn't just feel nice.
It works:
✅ On a loading spinner.
✅ In a push notification.
✅ As a tiny icon on a crowded home screen.

And it survives the worst test of all:
A PowerPoint.

At Goodspell, we don't ask "What do you like?"
We ask:
"What do you need to own?"
"What color are you willing to defend?"
"What happens when your brand shows up where you didn't expect it?"

Because consistency isn't pretty.
It's brutal.

So — what's one brand that just owns its color — not because it's unique, but because it's everywhere, all the time, without apology?
