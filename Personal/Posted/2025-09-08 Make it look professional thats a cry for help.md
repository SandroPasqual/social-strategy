"Make it look professional." That's not a brief. That's a cry for help.

I've lost count of how many times a client opened with that line.
And every time? We wasted 3 weeks going in circles.

A logo isn't art. It's a decision tool.
And if you don't tell me what kind of decision it should support, I'll guess.
And you'll pay for my guess.

Here's what actually works:
✅ Who are you when no one's watching? (Not the brochure version.)
✅ Where will this logo live? App icon? Invoice header? Billboard?
✅ What are we NOT doing? No gradients. No animals. Nothing that looks like that brand.

That's a brief.
That saves time.
That builds identity — not just decoration.

Most companies skip this.
Then wonder why their logo feels empty.

So — when you last worked with a designer, did you hand over a real brief… or just a vibe?
