Passive vs. Active Messaging — Know the Difference

The difference between a passive and an active message isn't about words — it's about intent.

Let me show you what I mean:

❌ Passive (descriptive):
"The Future of Work Playbook — a free guide for leaders navigating hybrid teams."
This tells you what it is.
But it doesn't ask for attention. It doesn't spark action. It just… sits there.

✅ Active (engaging):
"The Future of Work Playbook is finally here! After 6 months of research with 50+ remote-first teams, I'm giving it away free. Grab your copy before Friday — link in comments!"

This one creates context, builds relevance, and invites action.
It's not just information — it's an invitation.

Key takeaway:
Use passive messaging when you're labeling, categorizing, or documenting.
Use active messaging when you want people to see, feel, and do something.
Your goal shapes your message.
Not the other way around.
