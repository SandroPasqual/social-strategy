The most depressed appliance in the kitchen? The espresso machine.

Like a modern Sisyphus, it prepares with so much effort for its moment of glory, nearly reaching espresso perfection, and then... click. Brutal shutdown. All efforts, reduced to zero.

What's even more tragic: it never knows if there will be a "next time." It sits there on the counter, cold and empty, contemplating in darkness whether morning will ever come again.

Unlike the fridge, for example — at least that one has a continuous job, a clear mission, a sense of purpose. But the espresso machine? Condemned to this endless cycle of hope and disappointment.

Maybe that's why coffee tastes so intense — it's full of the machine's existential suffering. Each espresso is a small cry of despair, bottled.
