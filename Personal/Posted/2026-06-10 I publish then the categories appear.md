I publish. Then the categories appear.

Is this a blog post? A social post? A note? An essay?

The labels matter now because everything written assumes a destination. And every destination assumes an audience. And every audience assumes approval — likes, shares, the small dopamine signal that says someone else thinks this too.

We tell ourselves we're being social. And we are. But underneath that is something older — the need to synchronize. To send a signal into the dark and hear it come back from another direction.

You're not alone in this.

That's what we're really posting for. Not to be seen. To be recognized.

The tragedy is that the platforms figured this out before we did. And they built the machine around it.
