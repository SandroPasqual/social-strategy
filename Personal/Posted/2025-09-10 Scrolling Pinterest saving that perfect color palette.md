Scrolling Pinterest. Saving that "perfect" color palette. Closing the tab. We've all done it.

And we all pretend it counts as branding.

But here's the truth:
A color palette isn't a moodboard trophy.
It's a business decision.

And if you don't build it on strategy, it will fail in the wild.

Before you pick a single hue, ask:

- Does this palette reflect who you are — or just what you like?
A brand isn't "elegant" because it uses black and gold. It's elegant because every choice says so — consistently.

- Is it a system — or just three pretty swatches?
Primary. Secondary. Accent. Error. Success.
If it doesn't work across states and screens, it's decoration.

- Will it survive reality?
Check contrast. Test print. Run accessibility.
If it fails at WCAG, it fails your users.

Pretty colors don't build recognition.
Consistent, functional systems do.

So — have you ever shipped a product using a palette you found online?
Or do you always build from scratch?
