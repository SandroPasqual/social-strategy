Your logo is the last thing I design. Let me show you why.

A logo isn't a magic wand. It's the cover of a story that doesn't exist yet if I skip the hard work.

Here's my process, step by step:

The Why (Strategy)
Before I touch a sketch, I dive into the hard questions:
→ What's your core mission?
→ What are your non-negotiable values?
→ Who are you really talking to?
No soul, no memorable logo.

The How (Visual Language)
Once the strategy is clear, I build the moodboard. Not on taste, but on strategy. I ask:
→ Do these visuals reflect the values I've defined?
→ Will this style connect with the audience?
This creates the world the logo will live in.

The What (Logo)
Only then do I design the logo. It's not a random mark. It's the flag of everything I've mapped. It's meaningful because the meaning is already there.

The result isn't just a "nice" logo. It's a symbol you can defend, not just something you like.

Stop starting with the solution. Start with the questions.
