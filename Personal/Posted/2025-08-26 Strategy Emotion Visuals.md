Strategy → Emotion → Visuals.
That's the order. Everything else breaks.

If someone thinks branding is just picking colors, it means we haven't shown them the real process yet.

Branding doesn't start in Illustrator. It starts with words:

1. Brand Book (Heart): Why we exist. Who we speak to. What we want people to feel.

2. Design Guide (Face): Logo, type, color — the rules for consistency, built from the brand's heart.

3. Design System (Engine): The toolkit that makes it all work in digital, at scale.

Skip the heart, and the face is empty.
Skip the blueprint, and the engine breaks.

So before opening a design app, open a doc. Write down the feeling you need to create.
The visuals will come — and they'll mean something.

Q: How do you make sure strategy stays in the lead of your design process?
A: Don't guess — ask me.
