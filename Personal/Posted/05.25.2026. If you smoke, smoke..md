I'm not saying it's good for you. I'm saying that if you're going to do it, do it with full attention. Because the moment you split your attention — phone in one hand, cigarette in the other — you've lost both. You didn't smoke. You performed smoking while your mind was somewhere else entirely.

We've gotten very good at this. Doing things without being there for them. The phone trained us well.

The strange thing is: if you actually smoked with full attention, you'd probably smoke less. Because you'd have to decide, consciously, every single time. No automatic reach. No reflex. A real choice.

That's the only habit the phone accidentally taught us to break — by making everything else so thin, presence itself became radical.
