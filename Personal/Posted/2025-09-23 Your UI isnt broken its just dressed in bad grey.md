Your UI isn't broken. It's just dressed in bad grey.

You didn't pick a neutral.
You picked the default.

And now your app looks like every other "clean, modern" interface —
which is designer code for "we didn't think about it."

Here's the truth:
Neutrals aren't background.

They're the stage your brand performs on.
And most brands get it wrong.

✅ Pure black?
Not "sleek." It's void.
And on OLED, yes — it saves battery.
But in motion? It creates blur. Your UI feels sluggish.

✅ Flat greys?
Not "neutral." They're boring.
And when everything's the same grey, nothing matters.

✅ No accent in your neutral?
Then you're not subtle.
You're just… grey.

Smart UIs use accents in the shadows:
A whisper of blue → trust.
A hint of warmth → comfort.
A cool undertone → calm.

Not loud.
Just felt.

And they don't use 20 shades.
They use 8–10, with clear jobs:
This grey is for text.
This one for hover.
This one says "you're safe here."

So — what's the one neutral shade in your UI that silently does the hardest work?
