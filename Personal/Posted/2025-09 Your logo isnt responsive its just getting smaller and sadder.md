Your logo isn't responsive. It's just getting smaller and sadder.

You didn't design a responsive logo.
You designed one logo, then told Photoshop: "Just make it tiny."

And now it's a blurry mess on a phone screen.
Or a pixelated ghost in the browser tab.
Or worse — unrecognizable in a Slack message.

But here's the thing:
Scaling down isn't design.
It's surrender.

A real responsive logo isn't about shrinking.
It's about editing with purpose:

✅ Remove what doesn't matter — not what's inconvenient.
(Your tagline doesn't matter at 16px.)

✅ Amplify what does — the shape, the symbol, the aha.
(If it doesn't pop at 32px, it's not a logo. It's hope.)

✅ Respect the space — negative space isn't empty.
It's what keeps your logo from drowning in noise.

At Goodspell, we don't have one logo.
We have three:
One for the website (full story).
One for the app (essence).
One for the favicon (survival).

Not because we like extra work.
Because if your brand can't survive a browser tab, it can't survive anything.

So — when was the last time you saw your logo used at 16px… and it still looked like yours?
