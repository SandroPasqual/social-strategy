I talked to ChatGPT and it told me this:

Fear is a key driver for human progress. People move forward because they feel pressure, limits, and risk. Without that, nothing pushes you to change or improve.

AI doesn't have fear at all. It doesn't feel danger, loss, regret, or time passing. Nothing matters to it in a personal way. So it can't create its own goals or motivation. It only reacts to what humans ask.

Humans evolve because time hurts. Because the future can go wrong. Because mistakes cost something. AI has none of that.

Even with huge computing power, even with quantum systems, an AI still wouldn't feel fear. It would only calculate faster. The missing part is the internal push that fear gives humans.

Without fear, there's no self-driven evolution. Without a sense of time, there's no reason to act. That's the upper limit of what AI can be.
