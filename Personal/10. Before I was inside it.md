
The first time I saw people with smartphones — before I had one, before I understood what they were — I was genuinely confused. Not jealous. Confused.

I would lean in to look. Literally. What are they doing? It seemed urgent, whatever it was. Their faces said: this matters. Their hands said: I cannot stop.

Were they working? Arguing? Reading news that couldn't wait?

I couldn't figure it out. And I remember that confusion clearly — because it was the last time I looked at a screen in someone else's hands and didn't immediately understand the pull.

Now I don't need to look. I know exactly what they're doing. I do it too.

That moment of confusion was the last moment of innocence before the logic became obvious. Before I was inside it.
