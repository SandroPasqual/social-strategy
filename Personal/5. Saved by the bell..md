
When the phone buzzes mid-conversation, something happens that we don't like to name honestly.

Relief.

Not curiosity about who it is. Relief that the complexity in front of us — the face, the argument, the unresolved thing hanging in the air — has been interrupted by something flat and manageable. One notification. One vector. No texture, no ambiguity, no demand to actually be present for another human being's reality.

We pick it up because it's easier than staying.

We call it a habit. It's closer to an exit.
