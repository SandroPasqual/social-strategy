# LinkedIn 90-Day Campaign Strategy

##### LinkedIn Audience-Building Campaign  |  90-Day Plan  |  Launch: March 9, 2025

## 01  Strategic Context

### Primary Objective

Build a qualified LinkedIn audience that converts to inbound leads on a 6–12 month horizon, while generating the first qualified prospect conversations within 90 days through disciplined outbound.

These are two distinct objectives running in parallel — not the same objective with two timelines. Conflating them produces a strategy that underperforms both.

### What This Campaign Is

* A systematic demonstration of the Goodspell methodology through published thinking.  
* A targeted outbound motion toward high-probability prospects with identified transition signals.  
* The beginning of a compounding content asset — posts from month 1 remain discoverable in month 12\.

### What This Campaign Is Not

* A short-term revenue play. Content-driven inbound on LinkedIn does not produce signed clients in 90 days from a cold start.  
* A broadcast channel. Reach without engagement is vanity. Every post requires active distribution.  
* Optional. Without a live LinkedIn presence aligned to the Goodspell brand, the website has no traffic engine and the outbound has no credibility anchor.

### Audience — Primary Target

Professionals in visible transition: senior executives, specialists, and advisors leaving corporate roles to build independent practices. Identifiable on LinkedIn by behavioral signals within a narrow window.

| Signal | Where to find it | Action window |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Headline change (new role / 'Available') | LinkedIn search — 1st and 2nd degree | 72 hours |
| Departure announcement post | Feed — monitor 10–15 target accounts weekly | 48 hours |
| 'Open to work' frame or badge | LinkedIn search filters | 7 days |
| Company announcement — restructure / exit | Company page \+ news | 72 hours |
| New consulting LLC / website announcement | Feed \+ LinkedIn search | 7 days |

The action window is the period during which outreach lands as relevant rather than intrusive. After 3–4 weeks post-transition, the person has typically decided on their positioning approach or hired someone else.

### Audience — Secondary Target

Established operators: founders of 5–15 year businesses, $500K–$5M revenue, experiencing brand friction at pricing, hiring, or partnership conversations. Harder to identify by signal — reached primarily through inbound content that surfaces the Maturity Gap concept they recognize in their own business.

## 02  The 70/30 Mix — Definitions

### 70% Inbound — Content

Systematic publication of strategic thinking that demonstrates the methodology. The goal is not engagement for its own sake. The goal is that a qualified reader who encounters three consecutive posts concludes: this person operates at a level of precision I have not seen before in this category.

| Content type | Purpose | Frequency |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Framework post | Introduces a named concept (Maturity Gap, Zero Wasting Rebranding). Proprietary language builds recall. | 1× per cycle |
| Observation post | Identifies a specific tension in brand practice. No judgment of named brands. Evidence of field awareness. | 1× per cycle |
| Principle post | One declarative statement argued with precision. Short. No hedging. Tests the audience's alignment with the Goodspell worldview. | 1× per cycle |
| Process post | Shows how the methodology works in practice — a specific step, decision, or output. Demonstrates the system is real. | Cycle 2+ only |
| Synthesis post | Recap of a concept with a new angle. Serves new followers who missed the original. | Cycle 3 only |

### 30% Outbound — Direct

Targeted, contextualized messages to identified prospects within their action window. Not cold outreach. Not generic. Every message references a specific, observable signal from the recipient's LinkedIn activity.

| Outbound type | Timing | Volume |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Transition signal message | Within 72 hours of detected signal | 3–5 per week |
| Engagement follow-up | 7–10 days after a prospect comments on a post | 1–2 per week |
| Strategic comment (on prospect posts) | Ongoing — 3× per week on target accounts | Not a message — builds presence |
| Connection request with context | Before any message to 2nd-degree prospects | As needed |

## 03  Three-Cycle Structure — 30 Days × 3

### Cycle 1 — Days 1–30: Foundation

#### Theme

Establish the core vocabulary of the Goodspell worldview. Introduce the two primary frameworks (Maturity Gap, Zero Wasting Rebranding) and the positioning logic that separates brand strategy from brand decoration.

#### Inbound — Content priorities

* The Maturity Gap — observation post. The tension that defines the primary audience's problem. Week 1 Post 1:  
* Zero Wasting Rebranding — framework introduction. Named concept with 3–4 component logic. Week 1 Post 2:  
* Principle — 'Clarity of positioning outperforms visual refresh.' Argued, not stated. Week 2 Post 3:  
* Observation — 'What makes a brand confusing has nothing to do with the logo.' Week 2 Post 4:  
* Framework — 'Logic over aesthetics.' How design decisions become business tools. Week 3 Post 5:  
* Principle — 'The methodology is the credential.' Why process evidence outperforms portfolio evidence. Week 3 Post 6:  
* Observation — 'Why established operators rebrand at the wrong time.' Week 4 Post 7:  
* Synthesis — Recap of the Maturity Gap with new angle. Serves new followers. Week 4 Post 8:

#### Outbound — Cycle 1

* Volume: 5–7 messages per week. 20–28 total over the cycle.  
* Target: Professionals with fresh transition signals — headline change, departure announcement.  
* Message structure: specific observation → named tension → single open question. No pitch. No meeting request.  
* Strategic comments: 3× per week on posts from target-profile accounts. Not generic praise — a precise addition to their argument.

#### Success metrics — Day 30

* Post engagement: 40+ cumulative reactions across 8 posts.  
* Comments: 10+ substantive comments (not emoji reactions).  
* Outbound response rate: 15%+ (3–4 replies from 20–28 messages).  
* Profile views from target segment: visible uplift in LinkedIn analytics.  
* Connections accepted: 20+ new relevant 2nd-degree connections.

### Cycle 2 — Days 31–60: Authority

#### Theme

Demonstrate the methodology in operation. Move from concept introduction to process visibility. The audience that followed through Cycle 1 now sees how the thinking translates into structured work.

#### Inbound — Content priorities

* A specific step from the GBS methodology — how a discovery brief works, what a positioning statement actually contains. Process post:  
* A real outcome from an existing client engagement, anonymized. Three sentences of context, two sentences of intervention, one sentence of result. Micro case study:  
* 'The Brand Architecture Decision' — when a single brand is the right structure and when it isn't. Framework post:  
* 'Brands that beg for attention have already lost the positioning conversation.' Principle post:  
* What the transition from corporate role to independent practice does to brand perception — and what to do about it. Observation post:  
* What a GBS document looks like and why it exists. The methodology in physical form. Process post:

#### Outbound — Cycle 2

* Continue 5–7 messages per week on new transition signals.  
* Follow up — once — with Cycle 1 contacts who replied but did not continue. New value, not a re-invitation.  
* Engagement follow-up: anyone who commented on a Cycle 1 post and has not connected — send a connection request with one sentence of context.

#### Success metrics — Day 60

* Exploratory conversations: 2+ scheduled or completed.  
* Inbound messages: 1+ person reaching out from content (not outbound).  
* Post reach: growing per-post average — target 15% increase vs Cycle 1\.  
* Outbound response rate: 20%+ as brand recognition from content begins to support cold messages.

### Cycle 3 — Days 61–90: Positioning

#### Theme

Declare the position with precision. Move from demonstration to perspective. The audience that has followed through Cycles 1 and 2 is now ready for firm, specific points of view that either qualify or disqualify them as clients.

#### Inbound — Content priorities

* 'Most brand strategy engagements fail because they produce deliverables, not systems.' Firm perspective post:  
* The GBS three-layer brand architecture — public brand, client engagement, internal methodology. Framework post:  
* 'The cost of brand confusion is not aesthetic. It is commercial.' Principle post:  
* The full Goodspell positioning in one post. This is the post you send when someone asks what you do. Synthesis post:  
* What a brand system that functions without the founder's daily presence actually requires. Observation post:  
* 'Why I work on fixed-price engagements.' The logic of known value. Perspective post:

### Outbound — Cycle 3

* Continue base outbound on new transition signals.  
* Qualified follow-up: Cycle 1 and 2 contacts who showed interest — direct invitation to an exploratory conversation. One specific question about their situation. Not a sales call framing.  
* Referral activation: contact all 6 existing clients. Not a generic check-in — a specific message that names one person in their network who might benefit from the Goodspell process.

#### Success metrics — Day 90

* Pipeline: 1 qualified prospect in active conversation.  
* Inbound messages: 3+ people who reached out from content.  
* Audience growth: 150+ new relevant connections from zero base.  
* Content asset: 24 posts published — a discoverable library of the Goodspell worldview.

## 04  Outbound Message Framework

#### Structure — Three Components

Every outbound message contains exactly three components. No more. No less.

| Component | Content | Length |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 01 — Specific observation | Something true and specific about their situation, visible from their LinkedIn profile. Not a compliment. An observation. | 1 sentence |
| 02 — Named tension | A tension or problem that the observation implies — one that Goodspell solves. Named precisely, not generically. | 1 sentence |
| 03 — Open question | A single question that invites them to reflect on their own situation. Not a meeting request. Not an offer. | 1 sentence |

#### Example — Transition Signal

"I saw your announcement about leaving \[Company\] after \[X\] years — significant move. One of the most expensive decisions at this stage is building a brand presence before clarifying positioning, because the audience you attract early becomes very hard to reframe later. What's your thinking on positioning for the independent practice?"

Three sentences. No pitch. No 'let me know if you'd like to connect.' The question does the work.

#### Example — Established Operator (inbound lead from content)

"Thanks for the comment on the Maturity Gap post — you described it precisely. The friction you mentioned at pricing conversations is almost always a positioning problem, not a price problem. How long has that tension been present in your business?"

This message works because it references a specific thing they said, names the real problem, and asks a question that only someone experiencing it can answer.

#### What Never Goes in an Outbound Message

* A credentials statement ('I have 25 years of experience').  
* A meeting request in the first message.  
* A link to the website or portfolio.  
* Generic praise ('loved your post,' 'great insight').  
* More than three sentences total.

## 05  Content Production System

#### Pre-Launch Checklist — Complete Before Day 1

| Item | Standard | Owner |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| LinkedIn profile headline | Strategic Positioning Statement from 0108 — adapted to LinkedIn 220-char limit | SP |
| LinkedIn profile About section | Goodspell voice from 0201 Tone of Voice — 3 short paragraphs maximum | SP |
| Featured section | Link to website (when live) \+ one flagship post | SP |
| Profile photo | Controlled environment — Category 03 imagery standard | SP |
| Banner image | Category 01 imagery — GBS document pages, lateral light | SP |
| Website live | Any version. Imperfect is better than absent. | SP |
| Posts 1 and 2 written and reviewed | Full drafts — not outlines | SP |
| Outbound list Week 1 | 10–15 identified prospects with transition signals | SP |

#### Post Production Format

| Element | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| Language | English (US) — exclusively |
| Length | 150–300 words for principle/observation posts. Up to 400 for framework posts. Never longer. |
| Opening line | A declarative statement — not a question, not a hook. The claim comes first. |
| Structure | Claim → evidence or logic → implication. No numbered lists as the primary structure. |
| Closing | One sentence that opens a direction, not a transaction. Rotate three CTA types (see below). |
| Visual | Every post has a visual asset. 1080×1080px or 1080×1350px. amber.98 background, amber.heading for headline, Instrument Sans body, Wordmark bottom-right. |
| Hashtags | 3 maximum. Never in the body of the post — appended after the last line. |
| Publishing time | Tuesday and Thursday, 8:00–9:00 AM target audience timezone (EST for US-primary) |

#### CTA Rotation — Three Types

Rotate these three CTA types across posts. Never use the same type twice in a row.

| Type | Function | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Opinion invite | Generates comments. Builds engagement signal. | "Where do you see this tension most often?" |
| Value offer | Provides something. Builds goodwill and saves the post from feed algorithms. | "The full framework in the comments." |
| Conversation opener | Qualifies directly. Low pressure. | "If this describes your situation, worth a conversation." |

## 06  Weekly Operating Rhythm

| Day | Action | Time investment |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Monday | Finalize post for Tuesday. Review outbound list — identify 3–5 new transition signals. Draft outbound messages. | 45 min |
| Tuesday | Publish post. Respond to all comments within 4 hours. Send 3–5 outbound messages. | 30 min publish \+ monitoring |
| Wednesday | Strategic comments on 3 target-account posts. Review previous week's outbound replies. | 20 min |
| Thursday | Publish post. Respond to all comments within 4 hours. Send 3–5 outbound messages. | 30 min publish \+ monitoring |
| Friday | Weekly review: engagement metrics, outbound response rate, new connections. Note what worked. Adjust next week's drafts. | 20 min |
| Weekend | Draft 1–2 posts for following week. Identify next week's outbound targets. | 60 min total |

Total weekly time investment: approximately 3.5–4 hours. This is the minimum for the system to function. Below this threshold, the feedback loop breaks and the campaign loses momentum.

## 07  Measurement Framework

#### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Why it matters | Target by Day 90 |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Outbound response rate | Validates message quality and targeting precision | 20%+ |
| Exploratory conversations completed | Direct pipeline indicator | 3+ |
| Inbound messages received | Validates content quality and reach | 3+ |
| Comment quality (substantive vs. emoji) | Indicates audience alignment | 40%+ substantive |
| Profile views from target segment | Validates positioning clarity | Measurable upward trend |
| Connection acceptance rate | Validates profile credibility | 60%+ |
| Qualified prospects identified | Pipeline health | 5+ |

#### Metrics That Do Not Matter — At This Stage

* Total follower count. Irrelevant if the followers are not in the target segment.  
* Post impressions. Reach without quality engagement is not a business signal.  
* LinkedIn SSI score. A vanity metric that correlates weakly with commercial outcomes.  
* Likes. A reaction from someone outside the target segment counts the same as one from a qualified prospect. They are not the same.

#### 30-Day Review Protocol

At the end of each 30-day cycle, a 30-minute review answers three questions:

* Which post type generated the most substantive comments from target-profile accounts?  
* What was the outbound response rate, and which message structure performed best?  
* Is the content attracting the right audience, or is engagement coming primarily from peers and competitors?

Answers to these three questions determine what changes in the next cycle. The strategy is a starting structure — not a fixed script.

## 08  Risk Register

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Site not live at campaign launch | High | Publish anyway. LinkedIn profile is the credibility anchor, not the site. Add site link when live. |
| Low engagement in first 2 weeks | Medium | Normal for a cold start. Do not adjust content strategy before Day 21\. Adjust outbound message if response rate is below 10%. |
| Outbound perceived as spam | Low if executed correctly | Three-sentence rule \+ specific observation eliminates generic feel. If a reply says 'not interested' — acknowledge and close. Never follow up more than once. |
| Audience growth stalls at Cycle 2 | Medium | Introduce process posts — concrete, specific content about the methodology in operation. Performs better than principles at the Cycle 2 stage. |
| No exploratory conversations by Day 60 | Low-medium | Activate referral motion immediately: contact all 6 existing clients with specific referral request. This is the highest-probability action for a first conversation. |
| Content quality declines under production pressure | Medium | Batching. Write two weeks of posts in one session. Never publish same-day writing. |
