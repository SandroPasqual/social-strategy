# 030109 Imagery Guidelines | Goodspell | OUTPUT
## 03010901  PAPO
### Purpose
Defines the visual language for all non-typographic content in the Goodspell system — photography, imagery treatment, and graphic overlay rules — so every image reinforces brand positioning without requiring a brief or the founder's approval each time a visual asset is selected or produced.
### Audience
Sandro Pasqual as founder and primary content producer at launch. Any photographer, designer, or content collaborator selecting or producing visual assets for Goodspell. The Logic Manager when onboarded.
### Process
Derived from 0301 Visual Identity System and confirmed brand decisions from the Phase 01–02 strategic foundation. The imagery system is one of the most visible differentiators of the Goodspell identity in a category saturated with lifestyle stock and aspirational photography. Every rule here is a direct response to what not to do in this category.
### Outcome
A consistent visual voice in imagery that is recognizable across touchpoints and executable with a phone and natural light — no photography budget required at launch.
This is a significant operational constraint that is also a strategic asset: imagery that requires professional production to execute is imagery that creates a dependency. The Goodspell imagery system is designed to be produced by the founder with a smartphone and a surface with good natural light. This constraint is not a limitation — it is a positioning decision.
## 03010902  Strategic Rationale
The brand strategy and design category uses imagery in two dominant ways: lifestyle photography (people at work, smiling founders, collaborative moments) and styled brand asset photography (brand books on marble surfaces, stationery on linen, business cards with perfect shadows).
Both of these conventions perform aspiration. They are designed to make the viewer imagine being the client — feeling the experience of working with this firm. They do not demonstrate capability. They simulate it.
Goodspell's imagery strategy is the inverse: demonstrate the methodology by showing it. The images are of the documents, the system, the work — not of the people experiencing it. The subject of every Goodspell image is either the GBS methodology in its physical form or the precision of the tools used to build it.
This is not an austere aesthetic position. It is a strategic one: in a category where every image performs aspiration, an image that shows real work at real precision reads as evidence of capability — not as decoration.
## 03010903  Three Approved Image Categories
Goodspell uses three and only three categories of imagery. Every image in the Goodspell asset library belongs to one of these categories. Images outside these categories are not approved.
### Category 01 — System Details
GBS document pages photographed on a neutral warm surface. The document is the subject. Its structure — the numbered sections, the document header, the precise typography, the density of the content — is what the image communicates.

### Category 02 — Physical Materials
The GBS binder as an object. The binder is Goodspell's most tangible physical asset — the complete methodology in bound form. When photographed correctly, it is the single most authoritative image the brand can produce.

### Category 03 — Workspace Precision
Digital precision at maximum zoom. A monitor showing a Figma token system, a type scale specimen, a GBS document in layout, or an annotated strategy page. The subject is the precision of the tool — not the person using it.

## 03010904  Composition Rules
These rules apply across all three categories. They are not creative guidelines — they are structural specifications.
The primary subject has clear visual priority. Achieved through focus, framing, and tonal contrast — not through graphic overlays or heavy post-production.
Negative space is structural. It gives the layout room to integrate the image without crowding. Minimum 20% of the image frame should be clean surface or background — not filled with subject matter.
Horizon lines and edges of documents align with the implicit grid of the layout the image will be placed in. Images that create visual tension with the surrounding layout structure are not used.
Cropping is defined at the production stage — not resolved in layout. For images used across multiple formats, the crop zone is determined before the image enters the library.
All Goodspell imagery at launch is produced by the founder with a smartphone. The iPhone camera in Portrait mode at natural window light produces results consistent with these specifications. The constraint is a feature of the system, not a temporary workaround.
## 03010905  Color Treatment
Photography is color-treated to harmonize with the Goodspell palette without being visually subordinated to it. Images are not tinted in brand colors.
Color grading: subtle warming of shadows to align with the Paper palette temperature. Reduction of competing hues — cooler blues and saturated greens are reduced. The result should feel consistent with the surface the image will be placed on, not filtered.
Color temperature: all Goodspell imagery shares a consistent warm-neutral temperature. Cool images and warm images are never mixed within the same layout or page.
Saturation: moderately reduced. The imagery sits calmly alongside the typography — it does not compete with it for visual attention. High-saturation photography is not part of the Goodspell visual language.
## 03010906  Prohibited Uses
People, faces, hands, or any identifiable human presence in any image. The methodology is the subject — not the people who produce it or experience it.
Lifestyle photography in any form: smiling founders, collaborative working sessions, people looking at laptops, networking moments.
Styled brand asset photography from the category conventions: brand books on marble, stationery on linen, business cards with dramatic shadows. This is the visual language Goodspell has explicitly exited.
Stock imagery of any kind. Every image in the Goodspell library is produced specifically for this brand.
AI-generated imagery as a primary visual asset. AI-generated images can be used for internal concepting only — never in published materials.
Images that introduce a color temperature, saturation level, or hue that is not consistent with the warm-neutral palette system.
Photography that mixes color temperatures within the same layout or page without a defined visual logic.
Images with visible watermarks, compression artifacts, or resolution below 72dpi for digital and 300dpi for print.
Any image that stretches, distorts, or crops the subject in a way that misrepresents its proportions.
## 03010907  Text and Graphic Overlays on Imagery
Text placed directly over imagery follows fixed rules. There are no exceptions.
Approved overlay — Solid field
A fully opaque filled rectangle using an approved color token (Ink or Paper), placed behind text. The field is never semi-transparent. Color is from the approved token system only.
Approved overlay — Edge gradient
A gradient from Ink or Paper to transparent, applied at the bottom edge of a hero image where text must occupy the same visual space. The gradient direction, opacity range, and color are fixed — not adjusted per image.
Approved overlay — Clean area
Text placed only in areas of the image where contrast against the text color is guaranteed. The crop zone and text placement zone are defined together at asset production stage.
What is never permitted
Text placed over complex or busy image areas without a protective field. Semi-transparent color washes that tint the image in brand colors. Graphic shapes, frames, or decorative elements placed over photography. Any overlay color outside the approved token system.
## 03010908  Asset Management
All approved imagery is organized, versioned, and maintained according to 030111 Asset Delivery & Governance.
No image enters production from outside the approved asset library without a review against the criteria in this document. The review is performed by the founder at launch — and by the Logic Manager when onboarded.
New images are reviewed before entering the library — not after they appear in published materials. A review after publication is a governance failure, not a workflow.
At launch, the Goodspell image library will consist of approximately 6–12 images produced in a single session: 2–3 Category 01 document shots, 2–3 Category 02 binder shots, and 2–3 Category 03 screen precision shots. This is sufficient for the website, LinkedIn, and all client-facing materials in the first 6 months.
