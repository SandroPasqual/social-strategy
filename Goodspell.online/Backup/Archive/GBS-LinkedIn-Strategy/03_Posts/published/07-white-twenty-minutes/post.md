## Post Body
You spent the first twenty minutes of every call re-establishing credibility that should have been there before you arrived.

Not because you're not credible. Because the presence didn't do its job before the call started.

So you do it manually. Every time. You explain the background, the methodology, the track record. You give context that a website or a LinkedIn profile should have already given. You rebuild from zero because there's no foundation that arrived before you did.

By the time the conversation gets to the actual work, twenty minutes are gone. The prospect has formed an impression from the call — not from what they found before it. And you've spent the opening of every serious business conversation proving you belong in it.

That twenty minutes isn't a presentation problem. It isn't a confidence problem.

It's what happens when the presence doesn't carry the weight before you enter the room.
