## **01 The Strategic Foundation**

### What This Strategy Is Not

Not a content calendar. Not a sequence of cycles a prospect follows from beginning to end. Not a system that depends on a reader who has seen everything you've published.

LinkedIn is not a book read from page one. A prospect enters at any point — post 1, post 7, post 12 — and makes a decision based on what they find there. The strategy must work for that person.

### What This Strategy Is

A systematic demonstration of one precise thing: that Goodspell knows exactly where the prospect hurts — before he describes it.

Not education. Not convincing. Not explaining brand strategy.

Putting a finger on a wound the prospect has been living with, naming it with enough precision that he feels recognized — and then letting him decide.

The selection is done by the message, not by research into who is changing their headline.

---

## **02 The Core Insight**

People do not want a brand. They want to stop explaining themselves every time they walk into a room.

The prospect knows what he is worth. He knows what he has built. The only thing missing is a presence that says it clearly — before he speaks, before they ask, before the first meeting.

The battle is won or lost before the first conversation. Everything that happens before he opens his mouth — Google, LinkedIn, the website — is either working for him or against him. He is not in the room when that decision is made. His presence is.

Goodspell does not build brands. It extracts what already exists — what he does, how he thinks, what separates him — and makes it visible before he speaks.

### Zero Waste

This is both the process and the promise.

Nothing is invented. Nothing is added for aesthetic reasons. Every visual decision comes from strategy. Every word comes from the reality of his business. Every element of the presence has a function.

The client pays once for what is real. Fixed price — $6,000 or $12,000 — is itself the first signal of this principle. No infinite revision rounds. No options to choose from. No creative for the sake of creative.

Zero Waste means the work holds. Because it was never decoration.

## **03 The Two Audiences — Revised**

### Primary — The Established Operator

Founders of serious businesses — 5–15 years of operation, $300K–$5M revenue — whose presence has not kept pace with the actual quality of the work.

He is not looking to build something. He is looking to fix something specific. The budget exists. The decision depends on the moment when the friction becomes visible enough to act on.

He does not need three posts to confirm the problem. He needs one post that names it precisely enough that he thinks — this person has been in my situation.

**What he feels but cannot articulate:**

The pricing conversation that should not have happened. The good candidate who chose another firm after seeing the website. The partnership that stalled without explanation. The link sent before a call with a silent apology behind it.

He does not know he has a brand problem. He knows he has a friction problem. The post that names the friction — not the brand concept — is the post that reaches him.

### Secondary — The Professional Launching

Experienced professionals building an independent practice. Architects, engineers, lawyers, financial advisors, senior consultants.

He is not open to work. He does not announce his departure as failure. He plans, decides, and launches — and arrives at the moment of building his presence with a clear ambition: to be recognized at the level of the work from day one, not rebuild in 18 months.

He wants clients who arrive already convinced. Employees who asked to work with him because they knew what he stood for. Pitch without apology. Investors who turn toward him.

He wants his professional life to function without friction — and he knows a presence built on strategy, not aesthetics, is the difference.

## **04 The Message — What Every Post Does**

### The Rule

One post. One wound. Named precisely enough that the prospect recognizes it without being told it is about him.

Not a lesson. Not a framework presented for approval. A specific moment of pain — described in his language, from his experience — that he has never seen named before.

When he reads it, he does not think: interesting. He thinks: how does this person know this?

That is the only reaction that matters.

### What a Post Is Not

- An explanation of brand strategy  
- A framework with numbered points he can take to Fiverr  
- A demonstration of Goodspell's credentials  
- A sequence that requires the previous post to make sense

### What a Post Is

A single moment from the space before the first conversation. Specific. Painful. Unambiguous.

The pricing conversation where the number was right but the presence wasn't. The morning he sent the website link before a call and felt it before pressing send. The hire who chose somewhere else — not because the offer was wrong, but because the outside didn't match the inside.

Each post extracts one of these moments and names it. The prospect does the rest.

---

## **05 The Selection Mechanism**

Goodspell does not filter prospects through research. The message filters them.

The prospect who reads the post and recognizes the wound — he qualifies himself. He looks at the profile. He reads more posts. He sees the website. He finds that everything confirms what the post told him.

The prospect who reads the same post and feels nothing — he leaves. No time wasted. No conversation that goes nowhere.

This is Zero Waste applied to acquisition. The right people self-select. The wrong people self-eliminate.

The profile — posts, headline, about section, website — is the cumulative argument. Not a portfolio. Not a case study deck. Evidence that Goodspell has seen this problem before, understands its cost, and knows how to resolve it.

---

## **06 Outbound — The Revised Logic**

Outbound does not hunt for transition signals. It responds to signals of intent that the content generates.

A like. A comment. A connection request initiated by the prospect. These are the signals — visible, real, generated by content that landed.

**The three-sentence rule remains.**

01 — A specific observation about their situation, visible from their LinkedIn activity. Not a compliment. An observation.

02 — The named tension it implies — one that Goodspell resolves. Named precisely, not generically.

03 — One open question. Never a meeting request. Never an offer.

The question does the work.

**What never appears in an outbound message:**

- A credentials statement  
- A meeting request  
- A link to the website  
- Generic praise  
- More than three sentences

## **08 The Objective**

One qualified conversation in 7 days.

Not 90 days. Not 30 days. 7 days — if the first post lands correctly and three to five outbound messages go out in the first 72 hours.

Not guaranteed. Realistic.

The condition: the message is precise enough to provoke an immediate response, not a three-week reflection.

## **09 What Remains From LI-0001 v1**

- Voice: declarative, evidence-grounded, calm  
- Post structure: claim → evidence → conclusion  
- Opening line: always a declarative statement — never a question  
- CTA rotation: opinion invite / value offer / conversation opener — never the same type twice in a row  
- Post length: 150–300 words for observation/principle. Up to 400 for framework  
- No bullet points in post body  
- Hashtags: maximum 3, appended after the last line  
- Publishing: Tuesday and Thursday, 8–9AM EST  
- The three-sentence outbound rule  
- Cycles as internal operational organization — not as the prospect's experience

---

## **10 What LI-0001 v1 Got Wrong**

- The sequential cycle logic assumed a reader who follows from the beginning. That reader does not exist on LinkedIn.  
- The transition signal monitoring assumed time and tools a solo founder does not have.  
- The 6–12 month primary objective was a content agency's promise, not a solo consultancy's reality.  
- "Open to work" is not a transition signal — it is an exit signal.  
- The prospect does not need education. He needs recognition.
