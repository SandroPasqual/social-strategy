## Post Body
The price was right. The presence wasn't.

You knew the number was justified. The work behind it was solid. The client relationship was good. Everything pointed to yes.

Then something shifted in the conversation. A hesitation. A question that shouldn't have needed an answer. A silence that lasted a beat too long.

The price didn't lose that conversation. The presence did — weeks earlier, when they looked you up and found something that didn't match the number you were about to say.

Buyers don't negotiate prices. They negotiate confidence. When the external presence signals a level below the ask, the gap becomes the negotiation. Not the work. Not the value. The gap.

You can't close that gap in the room. It closes before you arrive — or it doesn't close at all.
