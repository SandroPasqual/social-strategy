## Post Body
The mood board made sense at the time.

The images were right. The colors felt appropriate. The references were credible. You looked at it and thought — yes, that's the direction. The designer presented it well. The logic seemed to hold.

It was someone else's logic.

Built from visual references, not from the specific reality of your business. From what looked good in the category, not from what your positioning actually required. From aesthetic judgment, not from a strategic brief that didn't exist yet.

You approved it. The work was done. The system was built on that foundation.

You've been living with that decision since. Adjusting around it. Adding to it. Trying to make it feel more like the business it was never built to represent.

The mood board was the beginning of a presence shaped by someone else's interpretation of a brief that was never clear enough to give.
