## Post Body
The candidate had already decided.

Not in the interview. Before it. Somewhere between receiving the offer and walking through the door, they had done what everyone does — they looked you up.

You weren't there when it happened.

They spent time on the website, on LinkedIn, on whatever they could find. They were building a picture of the kind of place this was — the level of seriousness, the ambition, the quality of what they'd be associating their name with.

What they found shaped the questions they asked. The hesitations they had. The comparison they were already making to the other offer on the table.

The best candidates evaluate the brand before they evaluate the role. The presence either confirms the decision to join or creates doubt that the conversation has to overcome.

You weren't in the room when the decision was forming. The website was.
