## Post Body
You've known the first impression is made before the first conversation.

Not as a theory — as a felt reality. You've been on the other side of it. You've looked up someone before a call and made a judgment. You've decided, before speaking a word, what level of credibility to assign.

You know this happens to you.

And you haven't fixed it yet.

Not because you don't know what needs to change. Not because the investment isn't justified. Because fixing it requires stopping, which requires time, which is always the thing in shortest supply.

So the first impression stays as it is. The decision that gets made before every conversation — about your level, your price, your seriousness — keeps being made with outdated information.

You know what's happening. That's what makes it the particular kind of expensive it is.
