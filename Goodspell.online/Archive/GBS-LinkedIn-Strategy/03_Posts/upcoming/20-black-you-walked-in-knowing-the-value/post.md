## Post Body
You walked in knowing the value.

Not arrogance. Evidence. Years of work, a methodology that holds, results that are real and repeatable. The number you were about to say was right. You knew it before you sat down.

Then the conversation shifted.

A question that shouldn't have needed an answer. A pause that lasted a beat too long. A comparison to someone cheaper that you hadn't expected to defend against.

You defended. Calmly, professionally, with good arguments. You explained the methodology, the depth of the process, the difference between what you deliver and what they'd get elsewhere.

By the end you had justified the number. They understood. Some even agreed.

But you walked in knowing. You left having explained.

That shift — from known to justified — happens before the meeting. In the website they checked the night before. In the LinkedIn profile they scanned that morning. In the forty seconds that formed the frame before you walked in.

The value was never the question. The presence made it one.
