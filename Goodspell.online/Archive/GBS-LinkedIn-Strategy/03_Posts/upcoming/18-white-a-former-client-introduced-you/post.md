## Post Body
A former client introduced you to a prospect.

They meant well. The relationship was good. The work you did together was solid and they wanted to share that. The introduction was genuine.

But the words they used were not yours.

They described what you do in the language they had available — approximate, simplified, missing the precision that makes the difference between a prospect who arrives understanding the value and one who arrives needing to be convinced of it.

The introduction landed. But it landed at the wrong level. The prospect's first question told you immediately — they had been given a version of you that undersold what the engagement actually produces.

You spent the first part of the conversation correcting the picture. Politely. Professionally. But correcting it.

A client who loves your work will always refer you. What they say when they do is a function of how clearly the brand has given them the words.

The introduction was warm. The language wasn't ready.
