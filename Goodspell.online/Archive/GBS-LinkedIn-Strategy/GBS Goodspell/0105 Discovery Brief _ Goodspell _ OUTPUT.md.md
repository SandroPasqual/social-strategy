## 010502 Business Snapshot
Goodspell is a brand strategy consultancy serving founders, senior professionals, and executives who are at a moment of deliberate transition — entering new markets, launching independent practices, or repositioning established businesses to match the actual maturity of their work.
The firm operates as a solo practice led by Sandro Pasqual, with 25+ years of experience across brand strategy and design and a documented track record of 200+ projects delivered across the United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany, the Netherlands, Australia, and Canada.
Goodspell is launching formally in 2026 after a strategic pivot from design execution toward brand strategy consultancy. The methodology — Goodspell Brand Strategy (GBS) — is fully developed, documented, and operationally ready. The public-facing infrastructure (website, LinkedIn presence, case study portfolio) is in active development. The firm has no venture funding, no external investment, and no marketing budget beyond the founder's time and the platforms and tools already in use.
The primary operating market at launch is the United States. Secondary markets — Western Europe, English-speaking international — are served through the existing network and inbound channels.
Pricing is fixed and transparent: Brand Foundation at $6,000 (Tier 2) and Strategic Authority at $12,000 (Tier 3). There is no Tier 1 in the public offer.

## 010503 Objectives for this Engagement
Over the next 6–12 months, the goal of this brand strategy work is to support:
### 01050301 Objective 1 — Establish credible public authority in the US market
Build a brand presence that signals strategic rigor — not creative services — to a serious buyer encountering Goodspell for the first time. The website, LinkedIn profile, and all client-facing materials must communicate a level of authority consistent with the GBS methodology and the $6,000–$12,000 price point. A buyer who arrives from LinkedIn, reads the website, and downloads a document must leave with no doubt about the category this firm occupies.
### 01050302 Objective 2 — Generate the first three Tier 2 or Tier 3 clients within 90 days of launch
The primary acquisition channel in the first 90 days is LinkedIn outbound and organic content, targeting professionals in active career or business transition. The brand must be positioned precisely enough that outbound messages land as relevant and authoritative — not as generic agency outreach. This objective is the operational test of whether the positioning is specific enough to produce results.
### 01050303 Objective 3 — Convert the existing client network into qualified referrals
Six documented clients across six countries (Canada, United States, United Kingdom, Germany, Australia) have experienced the work at the strategy and consultation level and have provided testimonials that speak to strategic outcomes — clarity, direction, identified gaps, actionable roadmaps. None have been formally asked for introductions to peers at the level Goodspell now targets. The brand must give these clients clear, confident language to describe what Goodspell does and who it is for — so that a referral from Michael R. in Germany lands as "you should engage this brand strategist" rather than "you should talk to this consultant I used."
### 01050304 Success for this engagement
Success is a brand that the founder can use without hesitation in every sales context: sending the website link before a call, sharing a document after a conversation, posting on LinkedIn without qualifying the content. The brand works when Sandro does not need to explain it — when the materials have already done the positioning work before he enters the room.
Specifically: success at 90 days is three qualified alignment calls per week generated through LinkedIn content and outreach, with a conversion rate from call to engagement of at least one in four.
## 010504 Current Context and Constraints
These realities shape the strategy. They are conditions to work within, not problems to hide.
### Constraint 1 — No existing case studies in the new format
The portfolio that exists reflects design execution, not brand strategy consultancy. The case study format required to support the $12,000 price point — situation, maturity gap assessment, strategic decisions, business outcome — does not yet exist for any client. This is the most significant credibility gap at launch.
Strategic response: Goodspell documents its own brand foundation as the first case study. The process of applying GBS to the firm itself becomes a public demonstration of the methodology. This is not a workaround — it is the most credible proof point available, because it shows the system working on a real problem in real time.
### Constraint 2 — The testimonial evolution is an asset, not a liability
The existing testimonials were originally written with the language clients had available at the time — the visible language of design deliverables. At the founder's request, clients updated their testimonials to reflect the strategic dimension of the work they had actually experienced. Both versions are authentic. They describe the same engagement from different angles — the initial deliverable and the full strategic dimension of what was built.
This is not a credibility risk. It is a credibility signal. The clients did not change their assessment of the work — they confirmed its strategic substance when asked directly. The fact that they could do so accurately is the proof that the strategy was real, delivered, and experienced as such.
Strategic response: No defensive framing is needed. The testimonials stand as-is. The About page narrative does not need to explain or qualify them — it needs to establish the strategic context in which the work was done, so a new prospect reads the testimonials against the right frame from the start.
### Constraint 3 — LinkedIn presence does not yet reflect the repositioned brand
The founder's LinkedIn profile currently reflects the prior positioning. A prospect who searches for Goodspell or Sandro Pasqual and finds a profile that emphasizes design execution will experience an immediate credibility gap relative to the website and the GBS documents.
Strategic response: LinkedIn profile update is a pre-launch requirement, not a phase 2 activity. The 90-day content strategy cannot begin until the profile is aligned. A misaligned LinkedIn profile undermines every piece of content posted from it.
### Constraint 4 — Solo practice with no marketing infrastructure
There is no team, no CRM, no paid acquisition, and no content production budget. Every hour spent on brand-building is an hour not spent on billable work. The system must be designed to run on the minimum of the founder's attention — which is precisely what the GBS async model is designed to do.
Strategic response: Concentrate effort on one channel, one content format, and one outbound approach for the first 90 days. LinkedIn text posts (no design required, no production overhead) and direct outbound messages to identified prospects. No blog posts, no newsletters, no video until the first two clients are confirmed.
## 010505 Market and Audience Notes
### 01050501 The brand operates in a landscape where:
#### Market Note 1 – The category is saturated with aesthetic signaling and empty of strategic substance.
Brand strategy providers overwhelmingly compete on visual presentation and creative reputation. The language is homogeneous ("we tell your story," "brand that connects"), the process is opaque, and the deliverables are designed for approval rather than for use. A buyer who has been through one or two brand engagements is not looking for another version of this. They are looking for something structurally different — and they cannot currently find it, because it does not present itself as distinctly different.
#### Market Note 2 – Buyers default to "safe" when they cannot evaluate quality.
The brand strategy purchase is made under conditions of high uncertainty. The buyer cannot assess the quality of strategic thinking before they purchase it. They default to social proof (follower count, client logos, referrals) and presentation quality. This means that a firm with inferior methodology but superior marketing routinely wins engagements it should not. Goodspell must make its methodology legible before the sale — not as a sales tactic, but as a filter that attracts the buyers who can evaluate what they are seeing.
#### Market Note 3 — The transition moment is underserved at the strategic level.
Professionals leaving corporate roles to build independent practices, executives pivoting to consultancy, founders repositioning established businesses — all of these people have a specific, time-sensitive brand problem. They are not served by brand studios that specialize in startup identity work, nor by large agencies that require a marketing team to manage the engagement. They need a structured, documented, independently-executable system — exactly what GBS provides. This segment is identifiable on LinkedIn by behavioral signals, not just by title.
### 01050502 The audiences that matter most right now are:
#### Audience 1 — The Professional in Transition (Primary, 90-day focus)
Experienced professionals — architects, engineers, physicians, lawyers, financial advisors, senior executives, specialized consultants — who are at an inflection point: leaving a firm to build an independent practice, launching a consultancy after a corporate career, or repositioning an existing business to attract a different level of client.
What they care about: getting the positioning right from the beginning, not rebuilding it in 18 months. They have seen what happens when a practice grows without a strategic foundation — they have colleagues who have gone through it. They want a brand that commands authority from day one, not one that apologizes for being new.
What decision they are trying to make: whether to invest in a structured brand foundation now, while the positioning is still forming, or to improvise and course-correct later. The argument for now is that the cost of repositioning an established brand is significantly higher than building the right foundation at the beginning.
How to reach them on LinkedIn: They are visible at the moment of transition — announcing departures, publishing posts about building something new, changing their headline from "Partner at [Firm]" to "Founder of [Practice]." This behavioral signal is the outbound trigger. Outreach within 2–4 weeks of a visible transition announcement has the highest conversion probability.
#### Audience 2 — The Established Operator (Secondary, inbound and referral)
Founders of serious businesses — 5–15 years of operation, $300K–$5M revenue, 1–20 employees — who have outgrown their original brand. The business has matured; the brand has not. The Maturity Gap is present, measurable, and producing friction in pricing conversations, sales cycles, and team communication.
What they care about: not losing a deal they should have won, not explaining their price again, not spending another year with materials they are embarrassed to send. They are not looking for creative inspiration — they are looking for a system that removes a chronic, low-grade strategic problem.
What decision they are trying to make: whether the friction they are experiencing is significant enough to warrant a structured intervention, and whether any provider in the market can deliver at the level their business now operates. The second question is the harder one — they have been disappointed before.
How to reach them: primarily through referral from the existing client network and through inbound from content that speaks directly to the Maturity Gap. The blog article on the Maturity Gap is the primary inbound asset for this segment.
## 010506 Brand Challenges to Solve
### 01050601 Consistency
The brand currently has no governing framework across touchpoints. The website (under development), the LinkedIn profile, and any existing materials each describe the work in different terms, at different levels of specificity, and with different implied audiences. A prospect who encounters Goodspell through more than one channel receives an inconsistent picture.
The specific inconsistency that must be resolved at launch: the founder's design background is present in some contexts and absent in others. The strategic pivot is either owned as a credential or quietly omitted. Neither approach has been applied consistently. The brand must make a single, deliberate decision about how the origin story is told — and then apply it everywhere simultaneously.
The testimonials are not part of this consistency problem. They are authentic accounts of real work, updated by the clients themselves to reflect the full dimension of what was delivered. They are a stable asset. The inconsistency lives in the structural layer — the LinkedIn profile, the website architecture, the outbound messaging — not in the proof points.
### 01050602 Positioning
The positioning is not yet visible in the external presence. The GBS methodology is fully developed and internally documented. The manifesto is written. The pricing architecture is defined. None of this is currently accessible to a buyer doing a first scan. From the outside, Goodspell reads as a design practice with strategic aspirations — not as a strategy firm with deep design literacy. This is the inverse of the intended positioning.
The specific gap: the website and LinkedIn must communicate "brand strategy system" before "design quality." Currently, the design quality is the primary visible signal. The strategic depth is not visible at all.
### 01050603 Evolution
The brand has grown from a design practice into a strategy consultancy — a deliberate, founder-led transition grounded in 25 years of working at the point where strategy meets visual execution. The transition is real and documentable. What does not yet exist is a governing narrative that makes this evolution legible to a new prospect in a single, confident pass.
The origin story is a strategic asset: a practitioner who spent decades executing brand decisions at the visual level, and who built a structured strategic methodology precisely because that experience revealed what strategy does and does not produce. This narrative must be present — explicitly and consistently — in every context where a prospect encounters the brand for the first time.
## 010507 Strategic Focus Going Forward
### 01050701 Clarifying who the brand is for and why it is a credible, preferable choice
Goodspell serves two specific audiences — the Professional in Transition and the Established Operator — who share one characteristic: they need a brand system that operates at the level of their work, not a creative service that decorates it. The brand must make this distinction explicit, in language that the right buyer recognizes immediately and the wrong buyer self-selects out of.
The credibility argument is: 25 years of executing brand decisions at the visual level, applied to building a structured strategic methodology. The design background is not a prior career — it is the evidence base for the strategic conclusions. A strategist who has never designed anything is theorizing. A strategist who has spent 25 years at the point where strategy meets execution has earned the methodological conclusions they are now selling.
This framing must appear consistently in the LinkedIn profile, the About page, and every piece of content that references the founder's background.
### 01050702 Building a simple, repeatable narrative that the founder and referring clients can both use
The narrative must work in two contexts: spoken (a 30-second description in a conversation) and written (the first paragraph of the website, the LinkedIn summary, an outbound message). It must be specific enough to be memorable and simple enough to be repeated by someone who is not Sandro.
The draft narrative for testing: "Goodspell builds brand systems for professionals and founders who are at a moment of transition — launching a practice, repositioning a business, or entering a new market. We use a documented strategic methodology to build the kind of brand that holds its position under pressure — in pricing conversations, investor meetings, and senior hiring. It takes 4–8 weeks. The output is a system, not a PDF."
This narrative should be tested in 10 LinkedIn outbound conversations before Phase 02 begins. The response pattern — what generates replies, what generates questions, what generates silence — is the next round of discovery.
### 01050703 Defining visual standards that signal strategic authority without competing with client brands
The visual system must communicate: disciplined, precise, established. Not creative, not expressive, not warm. The aesthetic register of a firm that has made its decisions and is not seeking approval for them.
Specifically: the visual identity must be one clear step behind the brands Goodspell builds for its clients. It should never be the most interesting thing in a room that contains a client's brand materials. Its authority comes from restraint, precision, and consistency — not from visual distinctiveness.
The typography system (Syne at headings, Instrument Sans at body) and the color system (Ink / Paper / Slate, no chromatic accent) are already defined. The remaining visual decisions — logo finalization with asterisk mark in Syne, document system visual standards, LinkedIn visual template — are Phase 03 deliverables that should be informed by the Strategic Positioning Statement (0108) and the Messaging Framework (0202) before any final visual decisions are made.
