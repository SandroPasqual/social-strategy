## 010301 PAPO
### Purpose
Structures the complimentary alignment call and captures what matters most to the client in one place. It keeps the conversation focused on stakes, desired change in the next twelve months, and whether a Goodspell Brand Strategy (GBS) engagement is the right fit.
### Audience
For Goodspell strategists and the client to use together during the Discovery Call. It gives both sides a clear, shared structure so the conversation stays anchored in what actually matters, not in small talk or scattered questions.
### Process
Used live during the call: shared on screen, followed step by step, and completed in real time while the client answers (or filled in by Goodspell afterwards using the call recording). Before the call, we review the 0101 PreCall Questionnaire responses and do preliminary competitive research, so we can bring focused observations instead of staying at a purely exploratory level. As the strategist, you can adapt the wording of questions to match the client's language, but the structure and intent of each section should remain the same.
### Outcome
A clear, decision‑ready picture of what the client wants to change in the next twelve months, what is at stake if nothing changes, and whether Goodspell's systematic approach is the right match. The completed script feeds into Internal Research Synthesis (0102) and helps both sides decide confidently on next steps.
###### Instructions: When the Discovery Call is conducted via a digital platform, the strategist uses the transcript as the primary source of truth. Post-call, the strategist performs Independent Heavy Lifting to distill the raw transcript into the "Strategist's Synthesis" (010306). The raw, emotional language of the founder must be preserved in the notes to fuel the Strategic Narrative in Phase 02, ensuring no sterilization of the brand voice.
## 010302 Opening
Use this section to frame the call, set expectations, and signal that you have done your homework.
Thank you for taking the time to complete the PreCall Questionnaire. Based on your answers, we've already done some preliminary research so we can focus this call on what actually needs to change in the next 12 months.
Today, the goal is to understand what is at stake for you, what "success" would look like, and whether a Goodspell Brand Strategy engagement is the right fit. If it is, we'll outline what a formal engagement could look like; if not, we'll still leave you with a clearer view of your situation.
#### You can adjust this to your own voice as long as:
you acknowledge their effort in filling 0101,
you make clear that this call is about stakes and fit,
you promise clarity, even if you do not move forward together.
## 010303 Big picture
### 01030301 Main change in 12 months
What's the most expensive problem in your business right now that you haven't been able to solve — and how long has it been sitting there?
The primary transition is moving from an uncodified, founder-dependent model to a disciplined strategic consultancy with a self-sustaining brand presence. In 12 months, Goodspell must be perceived as a stable, authoritative firm capable of handling high-stakes projects for serious buyers — disciplined founders, engineers, architects, doctors — in the US and European markets. The brand must carry the weight of 25 years of strategic depth without requiring Pasqual to be in every room to explain it.
### 01030302 What makes it "worth it"
Six months from now, how would you know this worked — not in brand terms, but in your actual day-to-day?
Success is defined by the ability to enter a client's office — physically or digitally — supported by a brand system that justifies Tier 3 pricing without negotiation. It means having a visual and narrative identity that commands immediate respect from sophisticated stakeholders. It means the materials speak before Pasqual does — and the conversation starts at strategy, not at justification.

## 010304 Who needs to feel the impact
This section surfaces the real people and roles that should experience the difference this brand strategy creates.
### 01030401 People and roles
Who specifically needs to see this brand and immediately understand that you're the right choice — without you being in the room to explain it?
Complex Buyers: Disciplined founders, investors, and senior corporate leaders — particularly those with technical or corporate backgrounds who expect immediate logical coherence.
Future collaborators: The upcoming Logic Manager and any future team members, who require a codified system to operate independently without constant founder validation.
### 01030402 Real-life moments
Think about the last time you had to personally step in and convince someone who should have already been convinced before the conversation even started. What were you explaining — and to whom?
High-stakes inbound pitches: Meetings with disciplined professionals — engineers, doctors, architects — who expect technical rigor and immediate logical consistency from the first touchpoint.
Cold outreach and outbound: LinkedIn interactions and cold email sequences where the brand must signal 25 years of strategic depth without the benefit of a personal introduction.
Premium proposal delivery: The moment a client receives a Tier 3 proposal — the document itself must justify the investment through its visual and narrative authority, removing the founder's hesitation to present premium pricing.
Team onboarding: The moment a new collaborator accesses the brand system and can operate independently, without needing Pasqual present to validate every decision.
### 01030403 Current friction
If I were a serious buyer who just landed on your website or received your proposal for the first time — what impression would I leave with, and how far is that from what you actually deliver?
There is a complete Visibility Gap at launch. No website, no published materials, no external proof points — a serious buyer cannot find, evaluate, or trust Goodspell based on any available signal. The authority exists entirely inside the thinking. The friction is not aesthetic — it is structural: the brand does not yet exist externally. This forces Pasqual to compensate personally in every conversation, which directly limits pricing confidence and scalability.
## 010305 Readiness and constraints
This section tests how realistic and actionable a potential engagement would be.
### 01030501 What they've tried so far
Have you tried to address this before — with an agency, freelancer, or internally? If yes, walk me through what happened. If not, what stopped you?
Previous business development relied on an ad-hoc, uncodified freelancing model without a disciplined framework to justify premium positioning. This produced four recurring failure patterns: attracting low-leverage clients with limited long-term potential; entering markets where price was the primary driver; experiencing price pressure due to the absence of a documented methodology; and internal friction from the lack of a single source of truth, leading to repetitive explanations and dependence on personal reputation rather than a self-sustaining system.
### 01030502 Capacity for involvement
In the next 2–3 months, where does your attention actually go — and realistically, where does this work fit in?
###### You can follow up with:
###### Who else can support?
###### Who can unblock decisions?
The founder is prepared to allocate significant time during the build phase — up to 12 hours per day — demonstrating full commitment. The operating model, however, is designed to protect that investment: strategic input is concentrated in defined validation sessions, while execution runs independently. The goal is to integrate a Logic Manager as a co-pilot to unblock decisions in the founder's absence, and to deploy AI-assisted automation to maintain Goodspell's presence without requiring constant founder availability.
### 01030503 Making the process supportive
If we work together, what would make this process feel supportive rather than overwhelming for you?
The engagement must operate on a low-noise, low-interruption model that mirrors Goodspell's own client philosophy. Specifically:
All work lives in a single dedicated portal — the one point of entry for every document, decision, and deliverable.
No communication scattered across messaging apps. No unscheduled calls.
Strategic inputs concentrated in 2–3 focused Zoom sessions. Everything else runs asynchronously.
Feedback is annotated directly on materials — specific sections, specific decisions — not discussed in open-ended review meetings.
The process must protect the founder's cognitive bandwidth for high-leverage strategic decisions, not drain it through operational management.
## 010306 Fit and next steps
This section is for you, the strategist, to synthesize what you've heard and to test alignment before proposing an engagement.
### 01030601 Core problem as the strategist sees it
Goodspell has full internal strategic clarity and zero external credibility. The methodology is formed, the pricing is fixed, the manifesto exists — but a serious buyer encounters nothing. The central problem is not a lack of expertise. It is that the expertise is entirely invisible. Every sale currently depends on Pasqual being in the room to compensate for what the brand cannot yet do on its own.
### 01030602 Success definition as the strategist sees it
Within 9–12 months, Goodspell operates as a self-sustaining brand system. The website, LinkedIn presence, and core materials convert serious buyers at Tier 2 and Tier 3 pricing without requiring Pasqual to explain the methodology on every call. The brand carries the authority. The founder focuses on delivery, not justification. The first collaborator is onboarded into a system they can operate independently from day one.
### 01030603 Main risks if nothing changes
Launching with an underdeveloped external presence at top-tier pricing creates an immediate trust deficit. Every conversation starts from zero credibility. The risk is not slow growth — it is that the pricing requires authority signals the market cannot yet verify, which forces either price concessions or prolonged sales cycles driven entirely by Pasqual's personal persuasion. The outbound engine stalls. The Logic Manager has nothing to work with. The firm remains founder-dependent indefinitely.
### 01030604 Questions to confirm together
The following four points have been confirmed prior to the Discovery Call and are recorded here as established positions.
Positioning commitment — Confirmed. No engagement proceeds to aesthetics without validated strategic pillars. This is not a messaging stance — it is an operational condition, applied without exception to every client, including Goodspell itself.
Domain decision — Delegated to the strategist. The domain choice will result from the strategy, not precede it. A reasoned recommendation — goodspell.online — is expected as a deliverable within Phase 01, before any public-facing asset is built.
First proof point — Confirmed with boundaries. Goodspell is its own first client. The GBS process applied to the agency itself will be documented and published selectively — what is publishable will be determined in collaboration with the strategist. Confirmed mockups will be composed from approved content. Website content (strategy and copy) must be finalized first, before deciding what is exposed publicly. Goodspell's own aesthetic remains neutral and one step behind any client brand — a benchmark, not a visual competitor. Distribution runs on a 90-day LinkedIn strategy from launch.
Tier 1 — Internal unit of calculation, not a public offer. The $2,500 tier does not appear in the public pricing structure. Its existence is operational. It will not be positioned, marketed, or offered as a standalone entry point.
