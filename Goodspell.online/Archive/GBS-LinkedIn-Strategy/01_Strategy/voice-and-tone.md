# Voice & Tone — LinkedIn

## Register
Senior advisor in a peer conversation. Not a vendor pitching. Not a consultant presenting options. A peer who has seen this problem many times, has a documented framework, and is willing to be direct about what the problem is and what it costs.

## Attributes
- **Precise** — Every word is chosen. Claims are specific enough that they could not appear on a competitor's site without being false.
- **Declarative** — The voice makes claims. It does not suggest, imply, or hedge.
- **Evidence-grounded** — Every assertion is supported by a specific observation, data point, or documented example.
- **Economical** — Nothing that does not serve a function. Brevity is a discipline.
- **Calm** — No urgency that has not been earned. No enthusiasm that has not been demonstrated.

## Archetypes
Expert + Strategist, with Pragmatist as governing constraint. When in doubt, the pragmatist wins: if a sentence does not serve a concrete purpose, it does not belong.

## Formality
Professional-Casual — the register of a serious business conversation between peers who respect each other's time. Contractions are acceptable where they serve rhythm. They are not used to perform approachability.

## Structure
Claim → Evidence → Conclusion. One idea per paragraph. The paragraph is the unit of thought.

## Grammar
Active voice. Present tense. Subject-verb-object. The verb carries the work — not the adjective. No passive constructions where an active alternative exists.

## Vocabulary

### Use consistently
System, framework, foundation, infrastructure (not: package, bundle, offering)
Documented, structured, governed (not: organized, planned, managed)
Maturity Gap, Strategic Authority, Cost of Frustration
Serious buyer, established operator, professional in transition
Engagement (not: project, job, contract)
Investment (not: cost, price, fee)
The brand works (not: the brand looks good, the brand feels right)

### Never use
Authentic / authenticity
Story / storytelling
Passion, passionate
Transform / transformation
Journey
Empower / empowerment
Unlock your potential
Take your brand to the next level
Game changer

## Boundaries

### Avoid being
- Too sales-focused / pushy — no false urgency
- Too vague / generic — every sentence must carry a claim unique to Goodspell
- Too trendy — no expressions that signal awareness of current marketing discourse
- Too wordy — if a sentence can be shortened without losing precision, shorten it
- Too formal — no complexity to signal intelligence
- Too inspirational — the voice does not motivate. It informs, demonstrates, and concludes.

### Structural prohibitions
- Rhetorical questions as headlines — they signal uncertainty about the claim
- Lists where prose would be more precise
- Any construction that begins with "We believe" — the voice demonstrates beliefs through behavior, not declaration
- Superlatives unless substantiated
- Explaining the methodology before the reader has been given reason to care about it

## Three Tests Before Publishing
1. **Specificity test**: Could this sentence appear on a competitor's website without being false? If yes, rewrite.
2. **Evidence test**: Does this claim have a specific, identifiable support? If not, find evidence or remove it.
3. **Register test**: Does this sound like a senior advisor talking to a peer, or a brand trying to attract a client? If the latter, rewrite.

## Voice in One Sentence
Goodspell's voice is the written equivalent of a senior advisor who has already done the analysis — direct, specific, and calm about conclusions that have been earned through 25 years of documented work.
