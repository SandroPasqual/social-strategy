# Goodspell — Client Intelligence

---

## The Ideal Client

The ideal client does not search for "brand strategy."

Most of the time, they cannot name the problem. They only feel that the public image of their business no longer reflects the level at which they actually operate.

They have built something solid — results, accumulated experience, and market weight. But their public presence communicates either too little, or something generic, interchangeable, without distinction.

They don't need trends, visibility, or explanations about branding. They need someone who understands:

- what they have actually built
- what they risk if the public image falls behind
- how difficult it is to let someone else interpret their work publicly

**The problem is not the absence of a logo.  
The problem is the gap between reality and perception.**

---

## Three Profiles. One Tension.

### 35–40 — Competent and Ascending

In full ascent, still validating important decisions. Has built a solid career and is either launching their own business or in its critical early years.

Understands the value of excellent execution, but positioning enters territory they cannot evaluate with the same precision. Afraid of diluting accumulated substance with a choice that will follow them for years.

Consumes intelligent content, compares, notices nuance. Extends trust slowly. Does not respond to aggressive selling. Seeks clarity and confidence.

**How they find Goodspell:** through content they immediately recognize themselves in, precise observations, and discreet recommendations — not through explanations.

---

### 40–50 — Clear Results, No Patience for Spectacle

Has results. No longer has patience for performance. Has been through enough cycles to quickly distinguish real competence from presentation.

The problem is not the absence of success — it's that the public image has fallen behind the reality of the business. Pays attention to people who observe correctly, ask the essential questions, and don't try to impress.

Difficult to win. Very easy to lose.

**How they find Goodspell:** through repeated exposure, indirect recommendations, and sustained consistency over time.

---

### 50–60 — Building for Continuity

No longer focused only on growth. The questions have shifted: what remains, who continues, how does what has been built get transmitted forward.

Not looking for a branding consultant. Looking for someone who can bring order, clarity, and continuity to a mature business. Does not enter public conversations frequently — but observes reputation, how work is discussed, and the quality of recommendations.

When they appear, they arrive already half-convinced.

**How they find Goodspell:** through private recommendations, discreet sharing, and reputation built over time.

## Channel Implications by Segment

| Segment | Primary discovery path | What triggers contact |
|---|---|---|
| 35–40 | Own content, recognition | A post that names their exact situation |
| 40–50 | Repeated exposure, indirect referral | Accumulated consistency over weeks or months |
| 50–60 | Private referral, reputation | A private recommendation from someone trusted |

---

## What This Client Is Not

The client who chooses based on aesthetics and price — from the high-volume, low-price segment of the design market — is not the target. They make decisions without a strategic brief and are not seeking what Goodspell offers.

Targeting this segment does not produce the right audience. Active avoidance of that context is a positioning decision, not a selective one.
