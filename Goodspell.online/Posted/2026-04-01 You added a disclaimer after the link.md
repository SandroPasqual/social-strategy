You added a disclaimer after the link.

"It's not fully updated but take a look."
"The website is a bit old but the work speaks for itself."
"Ignore the design — we're rebuilding it."

One sentence. Typed without thinking. Sent before you could stop it.
That sentence did more damage than anything on the page it was apologizing for.

Not because it flagged an outdated website. Because it told the person on the other end exactly how you feel about your own presence.

Prospects don't need you to point out the gap. They find it themselves. What they're looking for is confidence — that what you're selling matches what you're showing.

The disclaimer removes that confidence before they've seen a single page.
The website wasn't the problem. The apology was.
