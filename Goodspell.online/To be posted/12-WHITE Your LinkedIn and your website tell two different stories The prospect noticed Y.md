Your LinkedIn and your website tell two different stories.

Different tone. Different positioning. Different version of what the business is and who it's for. One was updated last month. The other hasn't been touched in two years. Together they create a picture that doesn't add up.

You didn't notice because you never see them the way a prospect does — side by side, in the same sitting, trying to answer one question: is this person who I think they are?

The prospect noticed. They spent four minutes moving between the two. They found the inconsistency before you had a chance to explain it. They drew a conclusion you weren't there to correct.

Inconsistency doesn't signal a work in progress. It signals a business that hasn't decided what it is yet. That's not the signal you want sending before the first conversation.

You can't manage an impression you don't know is being formed.
