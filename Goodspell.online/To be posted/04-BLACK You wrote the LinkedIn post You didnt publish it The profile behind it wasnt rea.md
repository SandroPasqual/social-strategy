You wrote the LinkedIn post. You didn't publish it.

It was good. You knew it was good. The thinking was sharp, the argument was clear, the point was worth making.

But before publishing, you checked the profile. The headline. The about section. The last three posts. What someone would find if the post landed and they clicked through.

You didn't publish.

Not because the post was wrong. Because the profile behind it wasn't ready to be seen by the people the post would attract. You'd worked too hard to send the right person to the wrong place.

So the post stayed in drafts. The thinking stayed private. The people who needed to read it didn't.

This happens more than anyone admits. Not writer's block. Not lack of ideas. A presence that isn't ready to support what you're capable of saying.

The post isn't the problem. It never was.
