You sent the link. They didn't reply.

Not a maybe. Not a not yet. Nothing.

You followed up once. Professionally. The kind of follow-up that says you're serious without saying you're worried. Still nothing.

You told yourself it was timing. A busy week. A decision delayed. You've told yourself that before.

Here's what actually happened: they looked. They spent forty seconds on the website. They made a decision. And the decision wasn't about you — it was about what they found when they looked for you.

The conversation was alive until the link. The link ended it.

This is the moment most businesses never examine. The proposal was good. The relationship was warm. The timing was right. The only variable was what happened in those forty seconds when you weren't there.

That forty seconds is where the work is.
