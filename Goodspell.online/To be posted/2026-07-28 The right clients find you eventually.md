The right clients find you eventually.

Through referrals, through reputation, through years of work that accumulates into something people trust. They arrive knowing what you do and why it matters. The conversation starts at the right level. The engagement is straightforward.

The wrong ones find you first.

They find you through search, through LinkedIn, through a presence that signals something broader than what you actually do. They arrive with the wrong expectations, the wrong budget, the wrong problem. The conversation starts at the wrong level and rarely recovers.

You spend time qualifying them out. Time explaining why you're not the right fit. Time that the right client would have used differently.

The presence is not neutral. It is either filtering or it isn't. Right now it's attracting indiscriminately — pulling in everyone who finds you before the right ones do.

The right clients were always there. The presence just isn't pointing at them yet.
