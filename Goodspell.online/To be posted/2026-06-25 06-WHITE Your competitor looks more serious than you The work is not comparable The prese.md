Your competitor looks more serious than you. The work is not comparable. The presence is.

You know this. You've seen their website. You've read their LinkedIn. You've sat across from clients who mentioned them in the same breath as you — and you felt the comparison land in a way it shouldn't have.

The work isn't close. You know what they deliver. You've seen the results. On quality, on thinking, on execution — there's no real competition.

But the prospect doesn't know that yet. They're working with what they can see. And what they can see places you in the same category, at the same level, competing on the same terms.

That's not a market problem. That's not a pricing problem. That's not even a reputation problem.

It's a presence problem. And it's costing you conversations you should be winning before they start.
