Everyone knows what you're worth after they work with you.

The clients who stayed. The ones who came back. The referrals that arrived with real weight because the person sending them had seen the work firsthand. They know. They don't question the price, don't hesitate at the scope, don't need convincing.

Almost no one knows before.

The prospect who finds you cold. The introduction that arrives without context. The decision-maker who spends four minutes on the website trying to answer one question — is this person at the level I need?

They're working with what they can see. And what they can see doesn't carry the same weight as what your clients know from experience.

The gap between what people know after and what they see before — that's where business is lost. Not in the work. Not in the delivery. In the moment before the relationship exists, when the presence is the only thing making the case.

The work built the reputation. The presence should be carrying it.
