The opportunity came when the presence wasn't ready.

Not a small one. The kind that appears once — the right introduction, the right moment, the right person asking the right question. Everything aligned except one thing.

You knew it the moment it arrived. The work was ready. The thinking was ready. The offer was ready. But the presence — the website, the profile, the way the business looked from the outside — wasn't at the level the opportunity required.

So you hesitated. You bought time. You worked on the presence while the opportunity waited.

Opportunities don't wait.

By the time the presence was ready, the moment had moved on. The person had found someone else. The timing had closed. The window that had opened briefly had shut without making noise.

The presence isn't something you build when the opportunity arrives. It's what makes you ready before it does.
