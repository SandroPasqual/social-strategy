You dropped the price before they asked.

Not in the negotiation. Before it. In the way you framed the proposal. In the qualifier you added before the number. In the alternative you offered without being asked for one.

You didn't know you were doing it.

It wasn't a strategic decision. It was an instinct — shaped by every previous conversation where the number was questioned, every meeting where the presence didn't carry the ask, every moment where you felt the gap between what you were charging and what the outside world said you looked like.

The discount wasn't in the price. It was in the confidence. And confidence is a function of how much you trust that what they see before you speak matches what you're about to say.

You negotiated against yourself. Before they had the chance to.
