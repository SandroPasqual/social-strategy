The website says nothing wrong.

Every sentence is technically accurate. The services are listed. The process is described. The contact form works. Nothing on the page would give anyone a reason to object.

It says nothing at all.

Nothing specific enough to belong only to you. Nothing that couldn't appear, unchanged, on a competitor's site without being false. Nothing that tells the right prospect — in the first ten seconds — that this is exactly the place they've been looking for.

A presence that says nothing wrong and nothing specific is invisible. It doesn't repel. It doesn't attract. It sits in a category with everyone else who wrote something technically accurate and strategically empty.

The prospect who needed to find you — the one whose specific problem you solve better than anyone — passed through without recognizing themselves.

The website didn't fail. It just didn't do anything.
