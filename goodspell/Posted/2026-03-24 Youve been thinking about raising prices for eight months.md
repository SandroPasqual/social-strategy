You've been thinking about raising prices for eight months.

Not because you need to. Because the work justifies it. The clients who know you well would pay it without question. You've seen the numbers. You've done the math.

The decision is obvious.

But every time you get close, something stops you.
Not the market. Not the competition. Not the economy.

The website. The LinkedIn profile. The way the business looks from the outside to someone who doesn't know you yet. You can't charge at one level and present at another. The gap between the two becomes the first thing a new client negotiates.

So the number stays where it is. Safe. Justifiable. Below what the work is worth.

Eight months of the right price sitting in a decision you haven't made. Not because you lack confidence in the work. Because the presence hasn't caught up with it yet.
