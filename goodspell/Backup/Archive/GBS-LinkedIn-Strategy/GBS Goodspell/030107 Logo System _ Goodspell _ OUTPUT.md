# 030107 Logo System | Goodspell | OUTPUT
## 030107-01  PAPO
### Purpose
Documents every approved Goodspell logo variant, its construction logic, and the rules that protect its integrity across all contexts and contributors. The logo performs correctly in every context without requiring the founder's approval each time it is applied.
### Audience
Any contributor — internal or external — who uses, reproduces, places, or adapts the Goodspell logo in any format or medium. This includes the founder in everyday content creation.
### Process
Derived from 0301 Visual Identity System and the confirmed brand decisions: The Spell mark + Goodspell wordmark in Syne typeface, Ink on Paper primary, Paper on Ink reversed, transparent PNG background, no chromatic color on the mark.
### Outcome
A logo system that maintains full integrity regardless of who applies it or in which context — because the rules are explicit, documented, and do not require interpretation.
## 03010702  Construction Logic
The Goodspell logo is composed of two elements: The Spell mark and the wordmark. Together they form the primary lockup. Their relationship — spacing, scale, and weight — is fixed. It is not adjusted per application.
### The Spell
The Spell is the primary graphic mark of the Goodspell identity. It is a custom radiating mark — designed and drawn specifically for this system. It is not taken from any typeface, icon library, or Unicode character set.
The name is not incidental. Goodspell operates from a clear conviction: that precision, structure, and craft deployed with intention produce something closer to the right answer than improvisation ever can. The Spell is the visual expression of that conviction — a mark that radiates from a fixed center in multiple directions, embodying the GBS methodology: strategy, narrative, and visual identity converging from a single documented foundation.
The Spell is always Ink. Never colored. Never gradient. The moment it receives a chromatic color, it performs personality — which is precisely what the Goodspell visual system refuses to do.
The Spell is a proprietary mark. It is not reproduced from any external source, recreated in any design tool, or approximated from any standard symbol. The logo files are the only approved source.
### The Wordmark
Goodspell set in Syne 800. Syne's geometric authority — its structured confidence, its absence of decorative stroke variation — is the correct typographic register for this brand. The wordmark is a fixed asset. It is not live type set in Syne. It is a reproduced logo file.
The wordmark is never recreated by setting type in Syne, even at the correct weight. The logo files are the only approved source. Setting type in Syne to approximate the logo — even if optically identical — is a misuse.
### The Relationship
The Spell positioned to the right of the wordmark in the primary lockup. The Spell is sized to the cap-height of the wordmark. The spacing between wordmark and The Spell is fixed at one Spell-width. These values are encoded in the master files. They are not adjusted.
The Spell follows the wordmark — not precedes it. This is a deliberate structural decision: the name carries the identity forward, and The Spell closes it. Goodspell*

## 03010705  Clear Space
Clear space is the minimum protected area around the logo. No other graphic element, text, image edge, fold, or structural border may enter this zone.
Clear space unit: one Spell-width on all four sides of the bounding box of the full lockup. For the Spell-only variant: one Spell-width on all sides.
Clear space is fixed. It is not reduced for tight layouts. If available space cannot accommodate the logo at minimum size with full clear space, use a smaller variant — or do not place the logo.
In digital applications (website header, LinkedIn banner), the clear space is defined relative to the container edge — not the viewport edge. The logo never touches the edge of its container.

## 03010707  Approved Backgrounds
The logo is approved for three background types. The correct color version is defined per type.

Light backgrounds — Paper #F5F2EA and warm neutrals above L*80
Use the primary Ink version. This is the default application. All website pages, all client documents on Paper surface, LinkedIn posts.

Dark backgrounds — Ink #1C1B18 and Ink Mid #2A2720
Use the reversed Paper version. Website dark-band sections, document phase covers with Ink background, LinkedIn banner if dark background is used.

Photographic or complex backgrounds
Use only on areas of the image that provide sufficient contrast. When contrast cannot be guaranteed, place the logo on a solid color field — Ink or Paper — not directly on the image. No semi-transparent backgrounds behind the logo.
The logo is never placed on any background color outside the approved token system (030105). It is never placed on a chromatic background — no colored surfaces behind the logo under any circumstances.

## 03010709  File Structure
All logo files are delivered and maintained according to 030111 Asset Delivery & Governance. No logo file outside the approved delivery package is used in production.

For each approved variant, the delivery package includes:

SVG — master vector file for all digital applications. Primary format for web, presentations, and documents.
PNG with transparent background — high-resolution (2x minimum) for each approved background type. This is the format used in everyday applications: LinkedIn, documents, presentations.
PDF — for print applications.
EPS — for vendor applications requiring vector format other than SVG.

Minimum size files are provided as separate assets — not scaled down from layout. Scaling a standard file below minimum size produces an unacceptable result. Use the minimum size file.
