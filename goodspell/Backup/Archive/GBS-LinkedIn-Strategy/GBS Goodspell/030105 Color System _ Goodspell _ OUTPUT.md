# 030105 Color System | Goodspell | OUTPUT
## 03010501  PAPO
### Purpose
Defines the Goodspell color system as a functional, documented palette where every color is strategically justified, every role is defined, and every usage rule is explicit. Turns color from a matter of personal preference into a precise signal for hierarchy, context, and brand authority.
### Audience
Designers and developers applying color across website, documents, presentations, and branded assets. Any contributor who makes a color decision for Goodspell — including the founder in everyday content creation.
### Process
Derived from 0301 Visual Identity System and the confirmed brand decisions from the full Phase 01–02 strategic foundation. The palette was not chosen by taste or trend. Every color decision is traceable to a strategic rationale documented in 0108 and 0301.
### Outcome
A color system the founder and any contributor can apply consistently and independently — without requiring case-by-case approval. The system is calm by default. Color carries signal, not decoration.

## 03010502  Strategic Rationale
The Goodspell color system is defined by one governing decision: no chromatic accent. The palette is warm neutral + Ink. This is not a minimalist aesthetic choice — it is a strategic position.
The brand strategy market is populated by firms that use color as a personality signal: dark-and-gold for luxury, pastel for approachability, bright accent for energy. Every chromatic choice in the category performs an attitude. Goodspell's palette performs nothing. It signals that the firm's authority comes from the precision of its thinking — not from its visual personality.
The warm neutral range — toned toward Paper — communicates physical materiality, document precision, and restraint. Ink communicates authority without aggression. The absence of a chromatic accent is itself a differentiation signal in a category saturated with them.
Blue was present in an earlier logo draft. It has been removed entirely from the system. It had no strategic foundation — it was a default. No chromatic accent will be reintroduced without a documented strategic rationale confirmed against 0108.
## 03010503  Core Palette — Three Functional Colors
These three colors are the primary instruments of the system. Every other color in the palette is a derivation or a contextual extension of these three.

### Ink  #1C1B18
The primary color of the system. Used for all text, all headings, all CTAs, the logo mark, and structural borders. Ink is not pure black — it carries a subtle warm bias that keeps it within the Paper chromatic universe. The pairing of Ink on Paper is the governing contrast of the entire system.
### Paper  #F5F2EA
The primary surface. Used for all backgrounds in client-facing materials, the website, and public-facing documents. Paper is warm — deliberately distanced from clinical white. It communicates materiality, precision, and physical document logic. All other colors in the warm neutral range are derived from Paper's chromatic position.
### Slate  #7A8890
A cool-leaning mid-tone used exclusively in documents as a structural signal. Slate appears in document headers, NLS codes, metadata, captions, and table headers. It is never used in client-facing public materials (website, LinkedIn, outbound). Its function is to create a clear visual distinction between primary content (Ink) and structural navigation (Slate) without introducing a chromatic accent.
## 03010504  Foundation Warm Neutral Palette
The full warm neutral range is available for layout variation, section differentiation, and document surface layering. All values share the chromatic position of Paper — same hue bias, varying lightness.

Only the working tokens defined in the token table (030105-06) are used in production. The full palette is available for future expansion — not for ad-hoc use.
## 03010505  Slate Range — Documents Only
The Slate range is the structural color family. It is used exclusively in the GBS document system — never in public-facing brand materials.

## 03010506  Functional Colors — Internal Documents Only
Three functional tints for internal document use: Confirm (green-warm), Note (amber-warm), Alert (red-warm). These are warm-tinted — they do not introduce pure chromatic primaries into the system. They are never used in client-facing or public materials.

## 03010507  Approved Working Tokens
These are the tokens used in production. Hard-coded HEX values in files, layouts, or components are not permitted. All color decisions reference these tokens by name.

## 03010508  Usage Rules
### What color does in this system
Color signals hierarchy and context — nothing else. Ink signals primacy. Paper signals surface. Slate signals document structure. Stone signals secondary information. Functional tints signal operational status in internal documents.
Color does not signal personality, energy, or enthusiasm. There is no accent color used to draw attention or to signal interactivity. Interactive states in digital applications use Ink weight and underline — not color change.
### Prohibitions — without exception
Blue in any form. It was removed from the system entirely. No shade, tint, or variation of blue appears in any Goodspell material.
Any chromatic accent not in this document. Purple, green, orange, red in any brand application — whether as a headline color, a button, a rule, or an icon — is not permitted.
Gradient applications of any color in this palette. The system is flat. Gradients are not permitted.
Opacity reductions of Ink used as a design effect. Reduced-opacity text for decorative layering is not Goodspell's visual language.
### When in doubt
Use Ink on Paper. Every other combination in this system is a variation of this core pair. If a layout decision requires a color choice not covered by the working tokens, the answer is to return to Ink on Paper — not to introduce a new color.
