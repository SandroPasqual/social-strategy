# The Recipe — Every Post

Goodspell Brand Strategy | LinkedIn

## Structure
- **Line 1** — Image and first line of post are identical. The moment verbatim. No setup. No context. The prospect recognizes it or doesn't — in the first second.
- **Lines 2–3** — Deepen the silence. No explanation. No commentary. Let the moment breathe. One or two sentences that confirm you understand exactly what that moment looked like from the inside.
- **The middle** — The hidden reality. The only place where you introduce something he didn't see in that moment. Not a lesson — a perspective. What actually happened. Without telling him what to think.
- **Last 1–2 lines** — Stop at the conclusion, not after it. Name the category of the problem. Not the solution. Not Goodspell. No explicit CTA. The prospect who recognized himself already knows what comes next.
- **Hashtags** — 3, after the last line: `#BrandStrategy #Positioning #BusinessGrowth`

## Constraints
- **Length**: 150–180 words. Never more.
- **Tone**: Someone who has been there. Not a consultant explaining.
- **Test**: If the last line says what it means — cut it.
