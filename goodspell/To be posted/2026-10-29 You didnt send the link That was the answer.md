You didn't send the link.

The conversation warranted it. The person was the right fit. The natural next step was to share the website, the profile, the presence — everything that exists to do the work when you're not in the room.

You didn't send it.

Not because you forgot. Because in the moment before sending, you made a calculation — and the calculation said the presence wasn't ready for this particular person's scrutiny. That what they'd find wouldn't match what you'd been in the conversation.

So you kept the relationship in the room. In the conversation. In the place where you controlled the impression.

That's the most honest signal a presence can give you. Not the prospects who pushed back on the price. Not the candidates who asked the wrong questions. The moment you decided — instinctively, without overthinking it — that the link would do more damage than silence.

The presence told you exactly where it stands. You listened.
