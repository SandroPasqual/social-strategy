The prospect decided before you spoke.

Not during the call. Not after the proposal. Before. Somewhere between finding your name and showing up to the conversation, they had already formed a position.

You felt it in the first five minutes. The questions were too pointed. The openness wasn't there. The energy of someone genuinely evaluating versus someone confirming a decision they'd already made.

You ran the meeting well. You said the right things. You made a strong case.

It didn't matter. The case had already been made — by the website, the LinkedIn profile, the way the business looked from the outside at eleven o'clock the night before when they did their research.

Your presence was in the room before you were. It spoke first. It made an argument you didn't know was being made, to a jury you didn't know was sitting.

It made the wrong case.
