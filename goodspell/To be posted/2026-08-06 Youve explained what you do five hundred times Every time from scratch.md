You've explained what you do five hundred times.

Every time from scratch. Every pitch, every introduction, every first call — you find the words again. Sometimes they come easily. Sometimes you hear yourself say something slightly different from last week and wonder which version was closer to the truth.

There's no version that exists independently of you. No sentence someone could read before meeting you that would make the explanation unnecessary. No document, no page, no profile that does the work before you arrive.

So you do it manually. Every time. To everyone.

The cost isn't the time spent explaining. It's what the explaining signals — that the business hasn't yet found the words that work without you in the room to deliver them.

Five hundred times. The five hundred and first is tomorrow.
