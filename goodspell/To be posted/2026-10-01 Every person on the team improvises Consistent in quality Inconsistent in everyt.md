Every person on the team improvises.

Not the work — the brand. How they describe the business, how they present the materials, how they answer the question "what does your firm do?" in conversations you're not part of.

Consistent in quality. Inconsistent in everything the outside world sees.

Each person has built their own version from what's available — the website, the onboarding, the way they've heard you talk about it. Each version is genuine. None of them are the same.

The result is a business that means different things depending on who the prospect is talking to. The quality of the work is constant. The signal it sends isn't.

Without a governing framework — something documented, precise, and accessible — the brand is whatever the last person said it was. That's not a team problem. That's a systems problem.

Consistency doesn't come from hiring the right people. It comes from giving them something precise to work from.
