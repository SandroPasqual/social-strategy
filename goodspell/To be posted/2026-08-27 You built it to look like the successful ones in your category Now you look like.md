You built it to look like the successful ones in your category.

It made sense at the time. They were where you wanted to be. Their websites, their positioning, their visual language — it all signaled the level you were aiming for. So you aimed there.

Now you look like all of them.

The same color palette. The same structure. The same language about strategy, systems, and results. The same photographs of documents on desks. Interchangeable at a glance with every other firm that made the same decision at the same moment for the same reason.

The reference points that were meant to signal quality became the floor everyone stands on. Distinction requires something different — not something better-looking, but something that couldn't belong to anyone else.

You built the presence to fit the category. The category absorbed it.
