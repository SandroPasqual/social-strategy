You sent the link.

The conversation was good. The timing was right. Sending the website felt like the natural next step — here's who we are, here's what we do, take a look before we speak.

You felt it before you pressed send.

Not hesitation about the relationship. Not doubt about the work. A specific, familiar feeling about the gap between what you were about to send and what you wanted them to find.

You sent it anyway. Because not sending it would have been worse. Because at some point you stopped letting the feeling stop you and started sending it with the internal qualifier you never type out loud.

The feeling doesn't go away. It shows up every time, in the moment before the send, as a small signal about the distance between where the business is and where the presence says it is.

You've learned to work around it. That's not the same as resolving it.
