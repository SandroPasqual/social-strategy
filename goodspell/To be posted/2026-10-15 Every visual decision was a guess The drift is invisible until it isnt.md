Every visual decision was a guess.

Not a bad guess — an uninformed one. Made without a strategic brief that didn't exist. Without a documented rationale that was never written. Without a system that could have made the decision obvious.

So you decided by feel. What looked right. What seemed appropriate. What the designer suggested and you approved because it wasn't wrong.

The drift is invisible until it isn't.

Each individual decision was defensible. The accumulation isn't. Over time, the uninformed decisions compound into a presence that doesn't quite hold together — slightly inconsistent across formats, slightly off in ways that are hard to name but easy to feel.

The prospect feels it. The candidate feels it. The partner evaluating the introduction feels it.

Nobody points to the specific decision that created the gap. The gap is the sum of all of them.
