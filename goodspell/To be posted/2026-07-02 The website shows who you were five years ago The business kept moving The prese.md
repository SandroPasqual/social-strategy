The website shows who you were five years ago.

The language is from a different stage. The positioning reflects a business that was still building confidence. The clients described are the ones who came before the work got sharper, the pricing got higher, the thinking got more precise.

The business moved. Every year it compounded — better clients, harder problems, cleaner methodology. You know the distance between where you started and where you are now.

The website doesn't.

Someone who finds you today is meeting the version that existed before most of what makes the business serious actually happened. They're pricing you, categorizing you, and deciding whether to reach out based on a snapshot that belongs to a different chapter.

You didn't fall behind on purpose. The business just grew faster than the presence did.

That gap is now the first thing every new prospect encounters.
