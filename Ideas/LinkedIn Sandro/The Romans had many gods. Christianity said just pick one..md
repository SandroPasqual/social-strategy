The LLM market is in its Roman phase.

Dozens of deities. Each with its own temple, its own priests, its own documented miracles. Each demanding attention, loyalty, and a separate account.

It's exhausting.

And exhaustion always ends the same way.

One will eventually offer enough. Not necessarily the best — but the most complete, the most present, the most embedded in daily life. And when that moment comes, the message will be simple:

*You've been saved. The others led you astray.*

People will make the sign. The rest will be cast out.

Not because one model is truly superior. Because choice is cognitively expensive. At some point the brain stops evaluating and starts belonging.

And then it's no longer a tool. It's an identity.

Criticize someone's model and watch them defend it like doctrine.

The most advanced technology in human history. The same tribal software running underneath. Unchanged.

We've been here before.

The pantheon always collapses into one.

The only question is which god writes the history.
